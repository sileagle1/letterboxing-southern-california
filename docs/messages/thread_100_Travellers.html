From: rscarpen (RiskyNil@pocketmail.com) | Date: 2003-11-30 08:40:39 UTC
I never even heard of a personal traveller until I started meeting up
with letterboxers on the east coast. It seems to be MUCH more common
out there and any gathering with more than ten people is likely to
have several personal travellers in the area. But it's always a word-
of-mouth thing where you have to say or do something specicial to get
it.

Let's see.... Jay Drew requires an English salute or something like
that. I wasn't sure what that was when I met him, so I tried a
regular old USA salute which did the trick.

Music Woman requires you to answer a specific question that she'll
ask you. Actually, the question is where did she used to work, but
she gives you an interesting hint to help figure it out.

Juggler Mouse requires you to find a couple of previous boxes and
show proof of finding them. (Technically, I think Juggler Mouse has
one and Sir B has the other, but both require the finding of two of
the Redwall series before they'll give 'em up.)

Ever since I learned about personal travellers I've been thinking of
making one for myself, although I want to do something that makes
people feel rediculous in the process. You know--make them WORK for
it. =) I like to make my stamps difficult to acquire. My favorite
idea I've thought of so far is a two step process:

1. A letterboxer walks up to me and says, "Toro! Toro!"

I'd whip out a bright, red handkerchief I have in my pocket and hold
it up like a bullfighter. Then step 2:

2. The letterboxer must pretend to make fake horns with their fingers
and charge the hanky. A roar would be nice, but is optional. =)

Naturally, the PT would have to be of a bull or something equally
appropriate. =)

Carpe diem!

-- Ryan
