From: trentlaudes (trentlaudes@excite.com) | Date: 2004-01-20 04:38:43 UTC
Leslie,
The sheet magnets, which I get from Michael's, are fantastic if they
are affixed to a smooth metal letterbox, like an Altoids tin, and
placed on a very smooth metal surface, like a metal sign, or
underneath metal stairs. But this material is weak in other contexts.
There's a famous letterbox in San Luis Obispo that uses this magnetic
material on the round surface of a plastic 35 MM film canister which
is then stuck underneath a metal shelf, and the hold is not very
strong at all (though this particular box, in a very protected
environment, has lasted since 12/29/02). I copied the use of
refrigerator magnets from a California Bear letterbox.
--Buzzard
