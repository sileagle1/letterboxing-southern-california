The Playground and South Pasadena Series

From: trentlaudes (trentlaudes@excite.com) | Date: 2004-02-15 16:41:51 UTC
Leslie,
I ran out yesterday to complete the South Pasadena Series, and the
Playground Series. Please tell Claire that her "The Rose" letterbox
in Singer Park is my 100th find. I found my first letterbox exactly
1 year ago today--"The Eaton Canyon Letterbox"--found on 2/15/03.
This letterbox is still very much alive. Spacey Holley & Family beat
me to your first three South Pas. boxes, but since you placed number
four more recently, she missed that one, and it's the coolest one to
find. I've heard of people planting these kind of letterboxes but had
never seen one. I need to get me an LB planted in my beloved
Pasadena library before you do it.

I had hoped to spend today finishing the carving on my latest series
(3 1/2 finished, 2 1/2 to go), but I just saw that you planted two
more letterboxes in Pasadena's Las Flores Canyon. I'm heading out
after these boxes in a few minutes, and hopefully they won't take me
too long to snag (my wife is being extremely reasonable about this,
but I don't know how long this will last if I keep disappearing on
weekends to find your rapidly proliferating letterboxes). I'm glad
to see you are heeding the letterbox clue-writing standards and
informing people of possible hazards they may encounter while
searching for your boxes, though I've never before seen a warning to
beware of mythological beasts (or are they?!).
--Buzzard P15 F105 X15 E2
