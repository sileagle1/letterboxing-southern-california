Las Flores Canyon Letterboxes, Altadena

From: trentlaudes (trentlaudes@excite.com) | Date: 2004-02-16 01:38:02 UTC
I visited the latest Leslie letterboxes today in Las Flores Canyon,
and was 1st Stamp in both. "The Cobb Estate" LB terrain should be
rated as very easy, and is good for small children. This is a very
interesting romp around early 20th century (c. 1916) foundations,
walls, and the remains of driveways and lightposts. On a clear day,
the view is lovely.

The second stamp in this series, "Bigfoot Waterfall," is another
matter. The hike to this letterbox is through a gorgeous canyon, and
I met no one on the trail either coming or going. But I want to
emphasize to other LBers that they need to pay close attention to
Leslie's description of the trail. When Leslie says to "Wear good
hiking boots," believe her. When Leslie says, "The trail is narrow,
and the drop off is steep. There are portions where you may need to
hang onto roots coming out of the cliff for support"--BELIEVE HER!
The particularly bad part of the trail is only about 10 feet long,
but it is a bit scarey, and I wouldn't bring any children younger
than teenager along (unless you've grown tired of their company). As
a lone hiker myself, this bad patch of the trail gave me pause, but
I'm unnaturally stubborn by nature, so I pushed onward. Considering
the 150 foot drop next to this part of the trail, having a hiking
buddy would've only made it easier for the police to recover the
body. Most of the rest of the trail is less scarey than this part
(now pay close attention to what I just said), and is really cool.
For the letterbox, it ends at two large, strange, concrete structures
deep in the wilderness, with a series of rusted metal pipes coming
out of them. One of these concrete "bunkers" made a great place to
sit and stamp in. More adventurous letterboxers will love this trek--
I like being out in the sticks.
--Buzzard
