From: Gnosis 4-H Club (gnosis4-h@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2003-08-20 23:45:32 UTC-07:00
We have been using jumbo erasers from the 99-cent store. Except I seem to
have purchased all of them in my area so now we are trying out the less
impressive, non-jumbo erasers. We use these cheep (2 stamps for 99 cents in
the jumbo, 8 in the smaller) ones both for cost and because they contain no
latex, unlike most other materials I've seen mentioned. Since I have bad
reactions to latex and I do all the carving (far too much risk letting 7 and
8yos handle knives), I factored in the stamping mediums materials in ways
others probably don't need to worry about.

Dear Purple Iris,

I have tried lots of materials but MasterCarve is the best as far as
ease of carving, definition, and durability.

The stuff was designed by a stamper who was later seriously injured
in a car crash. She sold the material's formula to MasterCarve who
kept the motto "Carves like butter" on the package. Hope that this
helpled her pay her hospital bills. Kate at Stamp Luis Obispo told me
about that.

Stamp Luis is now carrying the thinner MasterCarve, which works just
fine for box stamps. I like the thicker stuff for my personal stamps.
I think I paid $9 for two 4"X4" squares.

Now, what do all of us use for carving tools?

I use the Speedball set and a 0.5mm Two Cherries gouge for details. I
think it has improved my otherwise poor carving. I also have a set of
Dockyard gouges that I am slowly coming to appreciate. I bought them
and the Two Cherries at Mountain Heritage Crafters' website.

Hope to see lots of you at the Oct 4th event,
Sir B
