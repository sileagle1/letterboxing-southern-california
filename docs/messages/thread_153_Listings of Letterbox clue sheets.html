From: gwendontoo (foxsecurity@earthlink.net) | Date: 2004-03-17 05:02:21 UTC
We received a "Contact the Placer" note from some one that was
researching info on Millard Falls and she wanted to know more about
letterboxing.I sent her an attachment linking LBNA site and also
gave her a thumbnail description of letterboxing. I became curious
regarding the listing so I searched Millard Falls, Jack Creek Falls,
Fossil Falls,Seal Beach and a couple of of other locations. When
ever the title of our letterbox matched a location of the same name
our letterbox clue page was listed. I knew that when ever a
letterboxer had their own web page I could find some of them by
doing a search, but I did not know individual clue pages from the
LBNA list of clues would be found there too. I always assumed that
when NPS located a letterbox on park areas it was because they had
cruised through the LBNA web site, but my guess is that they just
check internet search engines. If you have a problem with having
your letterbox clues available outside of the LBNA clue listing then
be advised that you should not use the location name as the title of
the letterbox. If you currently have a letterbox on the grounds of a
location that may not want a letterbox on it's property and it is
named after the location sooner or later it will get you a "C" count.
