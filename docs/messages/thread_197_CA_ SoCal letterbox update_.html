CA: SoCal letterbox update.

From: trentlaudes (trentlaudes@excite.com) | Date: 2004-05-23 23:04:31 UTC
Last week, my company put me up overnight in the Long Beach Hyatt for
a work-related event. As it happens, four letterboxes I had yet to
find are located within 10 minutes of this hotel (one is only a
couple of blocks away). I was able to get enough time away from work
to find "White by Day, Blue at Night," "I'm a Heron Two," and "The
Signal Hill Letterbox"--all by Don & Gwen (didn't have time for the
fourth). These are all typical Don & Gwen letterboxes; beautifully
carved, and artfully placed.

And in the last couple of days I was able to bag two new SoCal
letterboxes, "The Transformed Man" by Realroadrunner, and "Dogs and
Stars" planted by Q and Shannon. If anyone visits "The Transformed
Man," try and get there during the Brand Library operating hours--
it's a beautiful art & music library. "Dogs and Stars" is a pretty
steep, long climb for many, but the views are great. The logbook is
really nice as well; where did Q find it?
--Buzzard

From: Gwen and Don Jackson (foxsecurity@earthlink.net) | Date: 2004-05-23 16:46:03 UTC-07:00
Buzzard,
Thanks for the nice compliment. The two you left in Kernville were pretty cool. I kept thinking "Sports Coach" not stagecoach(DUH!!!!) and was totally blown away by your cool carving. Dos Locos of SLO had beaten us to them as they found them on 5/8. They didn't try for some of the gathering stamps though. We did a maintenance check on the Whiskey Flat trail and Bull Run Creek boxes. You will kick yourself, get ready,when I tell you that you passed within 100 feet of the #1 Bull Run Creek box on your way to and from the Buzzards Perch LB. It is very close to where you had to cross the creek. Oh well there is always something for next time and that is why we put out more for the gathering than you could hit.

Don
