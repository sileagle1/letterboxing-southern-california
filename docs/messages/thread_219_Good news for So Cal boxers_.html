Good news for So Cal boxers!

From: (californiabear@comcast.net) | Date: 2004-06-24 22:03:22 UTC
My wife has been planning a trip for us to go to Italy and thus has been spending all of her waking hours on the computer and taking any time I might have been on the computer. Bad news for me!!!

So how is this good news for you?

I have been spending a lot of time carving stamps lately and have been planting boxes all week down here in Orange County. There is a range of difficulty and distance to choose from. There are drive-bys and long walks. There are no-brainers and brain-benders.

These are the ones in place as of today and (as far as I know) they are all unfound:
Tiki - Sunset Beach
Duck Soup - Irvine
Corona Del Mars - Corona Del Mar
Cassini - Newport Beach
TARDIS - Newport Beach

I have one more in the can for possible planting tomorrow. I had to test the container and attachment device to be sure it won't drop out of its hiding place.

I have another pair (short series) in the pipeline that may go out on Saturday morning if I can find a good place to put them.

I have also planted a couple of dummy microboxes around Walt's Kingdom to see if they can stay hidden from the sweepers and landscapers before I put a real stamp in place so keep an eye out for those!

I have an idea brewing for an 11 stamp series with an interesting scheme to find them. Start calculating exactly how long each of your steps is...

PS: The trip planning has no end in sight so there should be more coming soon.

In my post yesterday, I mentioned possibly planting another box this morning but alas, the container I was testing failed in two ways. I discovered that:
1) Spray paint doesn't adhere well to the type of plastic that is soft and flexible and tends to come off on your fingers as well being very sticky to touch.
2) You can't use hot glue to hold magnets to plastic in an environment that is hot and exposed to the sun.

Back to the drawing board for that one. I'll see what I can come up with this weekend and try to get it planted on Monday.
