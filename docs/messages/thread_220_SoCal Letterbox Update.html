SoCal Letterbox Update

From: trentlaudes (trentlaudes@excite.com) | Date: 2004-06-29 06:40:27 UTC
Well, as was detailed by me in an earlier post, I was disappointed in
my search for the Phoebe's Cabin LB, though the hike was fantastic.
While in the city of Thousand Oaks, I was able to find the Sycamore
Letterbox, and was there also rewarded by a hitchhiker--The June
Balloon--apparently launched by Silent Doug, no less! Silent Doug,
check to make sure you have your car keys. I loved the clues for the
Sycamore letterbox, and it was a challenge to find. The stamp has
broken in two, but it's still possible to ink the two halfs and hold
them together to make the stamp image.

Two days later, I was the first stamp in Sierra Sally's "San Gabriel
Archangel" letterbox (which is planted close to my home). Here I
left "Ralph the Hitchhiking Letterbox," which I found in Kernville,
and which has travelled all over the U.S.

This weekend, I travelled to Newport Beach in search of a plethora of
newly planted letterboxes, and an old one. I found "Tiki"
and "Cassini," both planted by California Bear. Both are great
stamps. The Cassini stamp clues are particularly fun to decode; it
took me a while, but in the end the clues were dead-on, and I was
successful.

While on this trip, I also found "Catch the Castaways Curlew." I
stamped-in this letterbox one week after Amanda. Don & Gwen, if
Amanda hasn't mentioned it yet, the tree over this letterbox has been
cut down, and a stump is all that remains. The letterbox, however,
is still in place beside the stump.

Finally, I found "Three Birds of Kells." What can I say, these are
beautifully carved stamps of birds placed in a beautiful bird
sanctuary--perfect. I left a few of the Newport Beach area
letterboxes to be found another day; preferably a hot summer day in
Pasadena, when I want to escape to the cool coast.
--Buzzard

view, and lo and behold, it's so close to my work, I found it during my

Dr. Moriarty's personal stamp made its debut appearance this evening at
the new Huntington Beach Micro Box Lifeguard Station #2. (This is the
tiniest letterbox I've seen yet!) He still insists he is not a
letterboxer, even though he was the first find the box location. He let
my son stamp the new Moriarty image into the logbook. He wouldn't sign
his name, but when I was going to leave it unsigned, he handed me the
pen and suggested that I sign for him. So I did. He's hooked. Stubborn,
but hooked.

Couldn't retrieve #1 "The Bench": someone was actually SITTING on the
bench READING A BOOK! What was this person thinking! Benches are made
for housing letterboxes, not for sitting! We hung around for a bit, but
he wasn't budging, and we had to leave to go to pick up my older son,
so we'll return later for boxes 1, 3 & 4.

Thanks again! I found a new way of making the images I use for my stamps. I had been trying a technique of taking a computer image and adjusting the threshold setting in a graphics editing program to make it into a black and white image but that often gave unsatisfactory results.

What I have started doing is opening the image in Paint Shop Pro, creating a new layer then tracing the image on the new layer. When I am done tracing, I hide the layer the original image was on and voila! I have a line drawing of the image just asking to be carved! I also started using the laser/photocopy and acetone transfer. I used to use the pencil transfer method but I kept smearing the image while I was carving and ended up having to guess where some of the lines were supposed to be.
