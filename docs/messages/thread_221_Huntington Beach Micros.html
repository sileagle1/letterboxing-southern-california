I found all four this morning and am glad that I did as the parking fee for the State Beach is going up to $10 as of tomorrow.

I read about the trouble Periwinkle had with someone parking on the bench over box #1. Someone was also parked there this morning but got up and left when I sat down on the bench. It seems that people don't like being 'crowded' on a 6' wide public bench. I got what I wanted: a letterbox!

I drive past the Nude Surfer Statue where box #3 is every day and never realized that he was nude...and I mean like anatomically correct nude. There is a reason he is facing AWAY from the street. YIKES!

I would recommend going for these either really early in the morning or really late in the evening, possibly even after dark as two of them are in pretty busy areas.

BTW: I love Dr. Moriarity's stamp. I had a good chuckle at that one. It definately fits the name.

We picked up the other Huntington Beach Micros yesterday, and also Mr.
Tiki. A nice morning spent down by the beach PLUS a cute set of micro
stamps added to my book--can't ask for more than that!

Yes, the parking fee at the State Beach went up to $10.00, and there is
a separate $10.00 fee at the City beach (can't use the same state lot
parking ticket, we tried). But you can avoid the City lot by the surfer
statue by parking at the meters on the street. At the pier, we parked
in the lot just west of the pier that allows you to pay by the hour. I
think it was $1.50 per hour.

Enjoyed the crafts fair at the pier. Tip for reaching the pier
letterbox when others are in the area (probably most of the time):
adult stands at sink to wash hands to block view of child ducking under
sink.

Had an early lunch at the Taco surf. Again, it's handy to take a child
to view the Tikis so as to look less conspicuous when going for the
letterbox.

Thanks Lee & Nancy and California Bear!

Periwinkle, Dr. Moriarty and the Child (who refuses to adopt a trail
name).
