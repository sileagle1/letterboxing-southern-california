The Claremont Colleges

From: trentlaudes (trentlaudes@excite.com) | Date: 2004-07-25 22:27:17 UTC
Moderndulcinea,
Since I knew it would likely hit 100 degrees in the inland valleys
today, I was up at 6AM to head 25 miles east of Pasadena to seek "The
Claremont Colleges" series of letterboxes. I left my walking stick
behind, because I figured I'd be wandering around expensive,
private
graduate school grounds which would probably not require any stream
crossings, or treacherous loose-gravel trail descents (by the way,
you can find these sorts of fun wilderness experiences on my newly
placed Tiburcio Vasquez letterbox series).

I was right. These letterboxes are very civilized, as are their
subject matter: Four great novels, illustrated by four great stamps,
planted in four great locations. MD, how did you ever discover the
hiding place for your "dragonflight" LB, it is inspired?!

Besides great stamps, I was also rewarded on this trip with some
other gifts. First, there was the "Arrrg. . .Hitchhiker,"
which I
found in the "To Kill a Mockingbird" LB. Then there was the
flag pin
left in "The Flamingo Rising" LB, probably left by the 3 Dumb Blondes
who stamped in on July 4th. Finally, there was the Claremont
Colleges keychain, which someone put in "The Sandman"
letterbox. I
wasn't the first stamp, but no one else had taken it, so I helped
myself.

The campuses were beautiful, and the temperatures were cool, since I
started out so early. A security guard kept driving by me in his
electric golf cart, seeing me again and again all over the campuses,
but never stopping to investigate my business. I guess I looked
harmless with my backpack, and my bottle of water, and my colored ink
stamps lying out on various benches. Thanks for the great morning
letterboxing! I also left something behind in one of your
letterboxes.
--Buzzard P27 F165 X26 E3 HH1

Hey Buzzard! So glad you enjoyed the boxes. I think it's funny that
you came to Claremont to cool off...I've been driving to Pasadena
and hiking in insane heat. :-)

Re: First Finder Goodies. I had never been the first to a letterbox
before, so I had no idea that people placed little gifts in them for
first finders. (which is why I missed taking the awesome little item
left in one of your Eaton Canyon boxes!) The 3 Dum Blondes were the
ones who told me about that, after they found one of my boxes. So I
went and planted first finder goodies in all of my boxes, even
though some of them had already been found.

Cheers!
