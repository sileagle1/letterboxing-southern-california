Kathi,
First of all, welcome to letterboxing which is a family-friendly,
economical, artistic, brain-challenging hobby. Since you're located
in Altadena, you're fortunate to live in one of California's
letterboxing hotspots; despite the size of our state, there aren't
all that many areas that qualify for this distinction. Other
California letterboxing hotspots are the city and surrounding areas
of Long Beach, San Luis Obispo, Kernville, and San Francisco. There
are also quite a few letterboxes spread about in Orange, Ventura, and
Santa Barbara counties. (No doubt there are also quantities of
letterboxes in other northern California counties of which I am
ignorant.)

Most letterboxers post updates to their clues when they get a report
that the box is missing. I haven't always been as quick to do
this as
I should, but at the moment, all of my posted clues mention if the
box is missing, or has been reported missing (not the same thing). I
believe most of the letterboxes within 10 miles of Altadena are still
in place. Not finding a box is just part of letterboxing; you have
to accept the serendipitous nature of the prey you are pursuing, and
delight when the journey ends successfully. That said, you'll
definitely find some local letterbox successes with the following
letterboxing.org listed boxes: "The Other Canyons Series" (a
hot
hike during the summer), "Ranger's Arroyo Seco Series,"
"The
Halloween Series," or the newly placed "Tiburcio

If a letterboxer has carved their own personal stamp, then they
should stamp it into any letterbox they have found. If they are with
a group of letterboxers, each member of the group stamps their own
personal stamp into the stamp book. It's up to your troop's
interest
in the hobby if each member wants to create their own personal stamp,
or create one that represents the whole troop.

Before going out letterboxing, you should read the letterboxing FAQ
located at:

http://www.letterboxing.org/faq/faq.html

That will answer most of your questions. Good luck!

From: California Bear (californiabear@comcast.net) | Date: 2004-07-28 07:03:27 UTC-07:00
I think letterboxing is a great thing to introduce to a Scout group. It combines artistry (stamp creation) with navigation (following the clues, somtimes including compass work) and hiking.

There is a 4-H Club in the L.A. Area that has planted several boxes. I'm not sure how many kids are in that group but it seems like they all do this together. I've never seen a box that they visited so I don't know how they handle the stamp in process. I have seen groups of 5-6 where each person stamped in individually.

As for having a group stamp vs. individual stamps, I would lean toward individual stamps. While it is more work to create the stamps and takes longer to stamp in if travelling as a group, having your own stamp also personalizes the experience more than all of them having to share the same stamp.

A suggestion to make the stamp creation process easier is to use fun foam glued to a block of wood. Shapes can be cut out with scissors which is much safer than giving an Exacto knife or sharp carving tool to the kids. You can find Fun Foam at craft stores like Michaels for something on the order of $0.80 for an 8x10 sheet.

From: moderndulcinea (moderndulcinea@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-07-29 17:23:04 UTC
Welcome!

In terms of kid-friendly boxes in your area, I'd recommend the
Descanso Gardens boxes. They seem very popular with kids and are
located in a large, pretty area where there are often school bus
trips. Also, at least two of the boxes are located around the "fairy
forest" in Descanso, which means the kids can also look for fairies
and stone squirrels and things that the are hidden in the Gardens as
well.

Re: finding boxes that haven't gone missing...check the date that
the box was placed, or look for a "last reported" date. If either
date is within a couple of months of the day you look, your chances
are pretty good that its still there. I've searched for maybe 30 or
so boxes, and only a handful were missing. I don't think a lot of
them go missing...it's just that when one does, people post about it
a lot because it's so sad!

Hope that helps a little!
