Kathi,
As you can imagine, most letterbox stampbooks are small in size. This
is a necessity given the clandestine nature of letterboxes, which
require that as small a waterproof container as possible be hidden to
help
prevent disclosure. My personal stamp is about 2 inches square, and
has more or less fitted every stamp book I've encountered, but I
wouldn't recommend going much above this size for a personal
stamp.
(I know of a family stamp much larger than this size [S.P.C.A.]; but
this stamp is a work of art, and I think not so very practical for a
stamp-in to the modern, smaller, micro-boxes of today's
letterboxes.)
--Buzzard



Re: How big are stamps?

From: rscarpen (RiskyNil@pocketmail.com) | Date: 2004-07-29 06:19:33 UTC
Some microboxes might require signature stamps as small as 1x1 inch
to fit. Most logbooks will usually handle 2x2 inches pretty well,
though.

Some people have two signature stamps--one extra small for those
microboxes--and a more 'normal-sized' (I used this term loosely
here) stamp for the more normal-sized boxes. =)

And then some people carve large stamps, but then just stamp a
portion of it if the logbook is small. So if you've carved an
animal, you could just stamp the head--or something like that.
*shrug*

Whatever suits your fancy! =)
