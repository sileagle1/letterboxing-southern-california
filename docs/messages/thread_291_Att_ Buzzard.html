From: trentlaudes (trentlaudes@excite.com) | Date: 2004-09-02 14:29:48 UTC
Yes Jam,
The bridge is often used for commercials, especially car commercials.
Because the bridge is curved, which is very unusual, it makes for a
particularly dramatic shot of a car swooping around a bend. It's one
of the very few curved bridges in the world. If you stop to think
about it, bridges are usually just built straight across a gorge; the
shortest route for an expensive structure. In this case, the ground
of the arroyo underneath the bridge was deemed too soft in spots to
handle the load, so a curved bridge was necessary to take advantage
of harder islands of soil. I've seen a photo taken in the 1920s of
the barnstorming pilot of a Jenny flying his plane underneath the
bridge, through the central span. And of course, the bandito
Tiburcio Vasquez and his gang rode their horses underneath this
bridge, 70 years before its construction, during their escape from
the Repetto Ranch raid.
--Buzzard
