Doughnut, Moody Cat, Watermelon, and Jelly Bean,

French Hen and I took another crack at your "FIGure It Out" mystery
box, and found it this time!! It so well blended in with the black
soil, I'm not sure that the first time I just missed it because of
the camouflage.

It was great meeting you at the SLO event. It was so kind of your
husband to help me look for my lost glasses, which we found. I told
your husband the lost glasses were probably located near another
letterbox that you all hadn't found at the time, and he accompanied
me there to help with the search, assuring me he wouldn't give away
the location to you.

At the SLO park, you mentioned to me that we should be thinking about
a Southern California LB event, and I agree. I think El Dorado Park
in Long Beach would be the perfect spot. It's a huge park with bike
paths, an archery range, a lake for fishing and boating, and a
wilderness area. Gwen & Don planted eight letterboxes in the park,
and I believe six of them are still there. Lots of room for more
plants.

At the SLO event, I didn't get a chance to do an exchange with all of
you, but I figured we'd probably be able to get together some time
down here, being locals and all. Your FIGure it Out stamp is
beautiful!! Nice carving!
--Buzzard and French Hen.

From: trentlaudes (trentlaudes@excite.com) | Date: 2003-10-14 01:52:28 UTC
Doughnut,

Last week, French Hen and I tried again to find "FIGure It Out," and
this time succeeded. I thought at our previous attempt that perhaps
some other letterboxer was stamping in at the time of our visit, and
that was the reason we couldn't find i; but after seeing how well
your LB blends in with the surrounding black dirt, I'm not sure that
we just didn't miss seeing it. This is a beautifully carved stamp!
For my stampbook impession, I used an antiqued, green metallic stamp
pad color, and the image looks like a 19th century book publisher's
monogram--gorgeous.

I've been to Frank G. Bonelli Park once looking in vain for a
letterbox, and though it is a large park that would work perfectly
for a gathering, El Dorado Park in Long Beach is much larger, and
more diverse; however, there IS a $5 per car charge. We'd have to
check with the park to see what deals can be had by renting a picnic
area. I know they rent these areas, and maybe parking for the
parties is included. I believe you'd fall in love with this park if
you saw it. It's located right off the 605 Freeway, just north of
the 405 interchange. Besides the Don & Gwen boxes in this park,
there are also 9 other Long Beach boxes, and more in nearby
communities, so (as you mentioned) it's a richer territory than the
San Dimas area (unless you and Purple Iris get ambitious in the
coming months). You make a good point about the expense of repeated
visits by us to plan and plant letteboxes, so this is something to
consider as well. Thanks for planting the beautiful letterbox!!
--Buzzard & French Hen
