Los Angeles Letterboxing this last Sunday.

From: trentlaudes (trentlaudes@excite.com) | Date: 2004-09-08 04:00:48 UTC
Sunday is generally my letterboxing day, and this last Sunday was no
exception. A couple of weeks ago, at the Critter's of Ennisbrook
Series: Salmon Rushdie, I had picked up the "Baby Beaver" HH. So I
figured the first stop on my "Westside" LB spree would be to Q's
Hollywoof Kennel Hitchhiker hostel. This was the first time I hiked
the Hollywood Hills when the air was clear enough for me to see ships
at sea; a gorgeous morning. Sure enough, there was another HH in the
hostel for me to trade with--and what an HH it was! I scored big
time! It was Legerdemain's #157 Lincoln Hitchhiker!!!! I only have
one other stamp from this legendary carver, K-Martha, an event stamp
from the SLO Gathering. What a beautifully carved and presented stamp
this HH is! I'm utterly thrilled! (Okay, enough with the exclamation
points; it's a cool hitchhiker.)

After leaving Runyon Canyon I headed south . . . oops, Wooly's
Letterbox is something of a Mystery Box: can't give its location
away. But the spot is magical (not in the Disney sense), and I was
happy to be 1st Stamp.

After Wooly, I back-tracked to the Hollywood Farmer's Market (every
Sunday on Hollywood Blvd) for a snack before heading over to UCLA to
seek Moderndulcinea's "Joe Bruin" LB. According to her clues
Moderndulcinea is a Bruin, and she planted her letterbox this last
Saturday. My wife is also a Bruin, and we were at UCLA this last
Saturday buying "Bear Wear" at the UCLA store. Hey Moderndulcinea, if
you saw a really tall bald guy with a beard, cool glasses, and a "Big
Dog Barbecue" shirt, that was me. It was so hot that day that in the
botanical garden sheltering the letterbox, I could well imagine I was
in a tropical rain forest.

I had planned to go on to the "Matador" letterbox at Cal State
Northridge, but the heat had sapped my strength, so I decided to
travel back from Los Angeles to the cool environs of Pasadena (locals
will snicker here; Pasadena is as hot in the summer as Kernville).
What a great letterboxing weekend!!
--Buzzard
