Working trip to Kernville

From: trentlaudes (trentlaudes@excite.com) | Date: 2004-10-26 03:25:12 UTC

I just returned from a 3-day working trip to Kernville. There was a
lot of work to be done, so there was very little time for
letterboxing, especially since the days have grown shorter since my
last trip in April. Still, I managed to plant two letterboxes, and
find two. The two planted letterboxes are located at the Kern River
Preserve in Weldon, a dozen miles from Kernville proper. And I
found "The Other One," and "Coccinellidae."

Let me tell you, the whole trip was worth finding Coccinellidae; this
is a spectacular hike followed by a spectacular letterbox stamp. Don
says this is the most beautiful stamp he has ever seen, and I agree.
During the walk up the canyon you pass waterfalls, mine entrances,
and negotiate fallen trees, all in an evergreen forest. And the
fallen trees and pine needles and plants are covered with masses of
ladybugs. There were spots where I could not see the forest bottom
for the red backs of massed ladybugs. I've never seen anything like
it, it's spectacular. Apparently this canyon is the winter home of
like every ladybug in California.

Thanks Pam & Scott for the incredible letterbox stamp placed in this
incredible location.
--Buzzard
