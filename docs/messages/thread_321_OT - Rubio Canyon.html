Purple Iris,
I'm familiar with the story of Rubio Canyon, which is not far from my
house, and have planned to hike up there, but haven't yet. According
to the latest edition of John Robinson's book "Trails of the
Angeles," a landslide in 1998 took out most of the waterfalls.
Robinson lists the hike as "easy" in going as far as the destruction,
but the link below to "Roy's Hikes in Angeles National Forest"
details the current state of the canyon. Check out hike number 25.
Roy's Hiking Page link: http://home.earthlink.net/~zeroy/

Purple Iris,
A few weeks ago I hiked the original Mount Wilson Trail in Sierra
Madre to Orchard's Camp, the site of a 19th century wilderness hotel.
Here there remains foundations and a enormous 1500 year old oak tree,
the oldest in the San Gabriels. On the hike, you pass through an oak
forest and spot small acorns on the ground. Standing before this
incredible tree, it's amazing to think that it came from one of these
little acorns that fell here in 500 A.D.
--Buzzard
