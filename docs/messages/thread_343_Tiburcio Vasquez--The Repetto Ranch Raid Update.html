Tiburcio Vasquez--The Repetto Ranch Raid Update

From: trentlaudes (trentlaudes@excite.com) | Date: 2004-12-11 23:27:11 UTC

I thought this 6-stamp series was such a good idea, and I carved more
than decent stamps, but as far as I can tell, with one exception, no
one has ever looked for them, even though they've been in place for 5
months!

I had a report that my stamp #2 in the series had gone missing
shortly after it was planted, but I just checked today and found it
still in place! I always did think it was in a bad location though,
so I moved it to a slightly better, but still not that great location
nearby. (Just couldn't find a good spot near here, and it has to be
here for historical reasons.) I also checked on my last box in the
series which is planted in West Hollywood at the spot where
Vasquez was eventually captured, and there was not a single stamp in
the book. Heavy sigh.... I mean Q, this stamp is within spitting
distance of your stamps (the worst carving, though)!

The stamps in this series are placed along the route of 19th century
California's most famous bandito's final raid, from the location of
the hold-up through the escape route, as he was being hotly pursued
by a sheriff's posse, eventually eluding the sheriff in the San
Gabriel mountains to the north.
--Buzzard

Hi Q!,
I had forgotten that you found #1 and saved the kittens, but that and
your report of #2 being missing was the only feedback I ever got on
this series. I was sure you were right about #2 being missing, 'cos
it was a pretty lousy place to hide a box, what with all that
exposure. I drove a couple of blocks down from the Disney Hall today
just to confirm that #2 was indeed missing, and I found a bottle of
ethyl alcohol buried underneath the shrubbery at the LB location. The
rock marking the location of the LB was gone, but I dug my gloved
hand around the spot and was astonished to find it under an inch of
dirt. As you can now see from the re-written clues on
letterboxing.org, I re-hid it at a different, but probably not much
better spot nearby. If you find a better hiding spot in that block,
I'll move the LB and credit this as a co-planted box.
