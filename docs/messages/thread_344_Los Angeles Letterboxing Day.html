Los Angeles Letterboxing Day

From: trentlaudes (trentlaudes@excite.com) | Date: 2004-12-12 00:44:38 UTC

Today I drove down the hill from Pasadena to the fair city of the
angels. First stop was the "Walt Disney Concert Hall" letterbox. This
building is very strange and beautiful. And as opposed to 20th
century buildings, there's about a million great places on it to hide
a letterbox. This was my first visit, though I'd driven by it before.
I had to circumambulate the building, and basically crawl all over it
to find the box, so I got a great tour. While searching for the LB, I
stumbled upon a geocache also hidden at the building site. I fear
one day this letterbox will be mistaken for the geocache, and the
stamp taken as a trade item. Fortunately for me, I had a brand new
colorbox silver ink pad, which was perfect for this stamp.

Next stop was the Hollywood Hills to find "To the Bat Cave," planted
by Q. This stamp and its location is way cool! The stamp is planted
at the cave used in the Batman television series as the spot where
the Batmobile zooms out of the Batcave. The stamp is fantastic, as is
the "Oof" stamp planted nearby. I was about the eighth stamp in the
books. Great job Q!

And being that this is Hollywood, while walking down the canyon from
Q's boxes, I ran into Jack LaLanne shooting an infomercial about his
juicer
--Buzzard

Nancy and I found the To The BatCave letterboxes about a month ago
(11/14) and while we we there, they were actually filming in the
cave. They were doing a film about 1930s chinese discovering alien
artifacts - to air sometime on the History Channel. It looked very
low budget. We were able to walk around back, and snag the box
without being caught. We then spent a good 3 hours searching for "A
Bridge Too Fingerprinted", but never found the correct area.

We also found Tiburcio Vasquez #3 and #4 a few weeks before (10/23),
and plan to go back for the rest after the holidays.

--Lee & Nancy

Hi Lee & Nancy,
How are you guys doing? I notice you've been busy planting
letterboxes; I'll have to take a road trip up there one of these days
while the weather is cool and have a hi-desert stamp-in.

I'm glad to hear you found my Vasquez #3 & #4. Moderndulcinea found
my #1, so that leaves #2, #5, and #6 that haven't been found (can't
personally vouch for #5, but it is the most difficult to get to).

I found Hamish Macbeth et al's "Passport to the Falls" letterbox on
4/26/03, which was planted on the same day as the "Bridge Too
Fingerprinted" box, but then searched in vain for this second box as
well. I suspect it's just further along the Angeles Crest HWY than I
was prepared to travel at the time (Big Horn Sheep country is pretty
high up).

I noticed your stamp in the "To The Batcave" LB; you all seem to get
down here a lot. I just planted two new LBs and will soon post the
clues on Atlas Quest. I hope to see you at this Northern
California Gathering in April that I've heard tell about. It'll be a
long drive for me, but much longer for you. I kick myself bi-daily
for missing the last San Francisco Gathering, but it was announced
without enough lead time for me to put together an S.F. trip. There
were some illustrious LBers at that gathering that I would have liked
to have seen again.
--Buzzard
