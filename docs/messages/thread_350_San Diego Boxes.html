Hi Everyone!

I took a trip to San Diego yesterday and saw some of the most
beautiful places that I have ever seen thanks to letterboxing!

The first box we went to was the Batiquitos Lagoon letterbox. I had
often seen this place from the I-5, but never really knew what it
was! This was a great box due to the glimpse of history that it
showed me of LB'ing (and geocaching) in S. California. The swing was
fun too! Thanks Buzzard for having adopted this one!

Then it was off to the Solana Beach Stingray Microbox, which I
couldn't find. Boohoo! I had a hard time following the clues even
though they seemed very straightforward. I'll have to try it again
some other time. Anyone got some pointers?

Next was La Jolla LB, which was a beautiful place right off the
coast. The sun was beginning to go down, so the contrast of the
golden cliffs against the vivid blue ocean was remarkable. There
was some foot traffic there but we managed to be insconspicuous.
I'll admit, though, that it took some waiting for the traffic to
clear!

We tried squeezing in one last box for the day... the Tea Time
Letterbox. Boy, what a perfect way to end the day!! This location
is probably the most beautiful place I've seen so far in SD! Lots of
greenery, spectacular view of the ocean, and by far the biggest
mansion I have ever seen! I imagine that's what the Gatsby mansion
would look like if it existed! We stamped in and watched the sunset
("We" meaning my boyfriend and I... I drag him along on these LBing
gigs with me).

Been to San Diego a lot, but never knew these places exsited!
Overall a very pleasant and fruitful day in San Diego. Where else
can you find 75-degree weather in the middle of December?

-GG
