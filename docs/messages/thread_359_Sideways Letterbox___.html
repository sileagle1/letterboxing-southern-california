'Sideways' fans go for taste of wine country
By Kitty Bean Yancey, USA TODAY


Room 234 at the Days Inn at the Windmill in Buellton, Calif., may
look like nothing more than a typical roadside rest stop two beds,
TV, not fancy. But now it's a tourist attraction.

The actors who play two pinot-swigging road trippers in Sideways
filmed motel scenes in the room, which rents for $69 a night and up.
Since the little movie's big success, "people come stay just because
we're in it," front desk manager Vera Villalobos says. "Some come
just to take pictures."

In fact, the film the most-nominated at this Sunday's Golden Globe
Awards and viewed as an Oscar contender as well has uncorked a
tourism swell in the Santa Ynez Valley, about 130 miles north of Los
Angeles and a 45-minute drive north of Santa Barbara.

At the Sanford Winery, where wine snob Miles (Paul Giamatti) opines
in Sideways about a sample's strawberry/asparagus/Edam overtones,
tasting-room manager Chris Burroughs (the long-haired, cowboy-hatted
dude seen patiently putting up with Miles and oafish pal Jack in the
movie) is hosting a stream of visitors. Some even changed vacation
plans and made a detour after seeing Sideways, he says. "It's great
for business."

And that Sanford vin gris that Miles evaluates in the movie (it's
just a fancy name for ros, by the way) has sold out.

In Buellton, the busy Hitching Post II restaurant a real-life local
hangout where Miles drowns his sorrows and falls for a waitress is
even busier. Sideways T-shirts ($14) are now on the menu there.

"Our wine country is a star of the film we couldn't have asked for
better PR," says Shannon Brooks of the Santa Barbara Conference &
Visitors Bureau. It's building on the buzz with "Sideways, The Map,"
a self-guided tour map spotlighting 18 film locations, including
wineries, eateries and Ostrich Land. It's free at 800-676-1266 or
santabarbaraca.com.

The Fox Searchlight studio, distributor of Sideways, has collaborated
on a whimsical 48-page guide to locations, plus wine tips and movie
dialogue. Published by Newmarket Press, it's sold at some film
locales (including the Hitching Post, where it costs $6) and may be
coming to bookstores soon.

Meanwhile, Santa Barbara-based Personal Tours Ltd. is offering
customized Sideways guided outings. One stop might be the
film's "Frass Canyon" winery, where Miles goes berserk after finding
out the book he has poured his soul into won't be published. In real
life, the vineyard is the Fess Parker Winery, owned by the Daniel
Boone and Davy Crockett star.

Because of the film, "the wine country which has been up and
coming is really getting attention," says Personal Tours Ltd.
director David Echols.

"For the Sideways tours, most of the people are not (wine)
professionals. They just want to relive the fun of that movie."
