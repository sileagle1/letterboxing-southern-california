SoCal Stamps this last weekend.

From: trentlaudes (trentlaudes@excite.com) | Date: 2005-01-25 06:37:19 UTC

All the trails in the San Gabriel Mountains are basically closed for
hiking because of rain damage, so that left me with no other option
but to letterbox this last Saturday and Sunday. Saturday I found
the "Horse Thief Canyon" Series in San Dimas. Those turkeys Lee &
Nancy beat me to first stamp the Saturday before. How dare they
descend to the lowlands to grab up our rightful boxes! I loved the
cryptogram clues to this series (which took me a full week longer
than Lee & Nancy to decipher).

On Sunday, it was off to Mission San Fernando de Espana for a
letterbox planted by . . . can you guess . . . wait for it: SIERRA
SALLY! I'm getting quite the California Mission education based
on Sierra's signature series. Of course, in this case, I
didn't have to pay the $5 to tour the mission first, which did
somewhat detract from the educational experience of my visit (it was
pretty from the outside, though). Thanks for the great stamp, Sierra!

Then I had to leave the mission and speed off after the "E.T.
Phone Home" letterbox in Northridge, planted by geishagirl. This
letterbox is planted in a park that was used in the filming of the
movie, and has some great views of the foothills.

After E.T., my well-planned itinerary took me to Cal State Northridge
in search of the "CSUN Botanical Gardens" letterbox planted by Parrot
Head jmo. Alas, perhaps because it is during winter break, the
gardens were closed; locked up tight; even though they are normally
open on Sundays. I contemplated breaking in, but though the fence was
low, it consisted of close-set, medieval-looking spikes, and I had
visions of Vlad the Impaler, so I decided to wait for spring term.

Four great stamps in two days is a windfall here in SoCal, so I had a
great time.
--Buzzard
