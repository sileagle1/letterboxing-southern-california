> Just to start a conversation here, How does everyone feel about the
> Snail Mail boxes? Purple Iris

I think it is a great idea. I look forward to the day when one of them will
show up in my mailbox.

I know some people are a little freaked by the idea of someone having their
address but it doesn't bother me. Then again, I am a male. If I was a female
who lived alone, I might be a little iffy about giving out my address to people
who are pracitcally strangers.

BTW: I am sorry for neglecting my own group. I have been sick for a
bit now and have pretty much been going home from work and going to bed without
checking my e-mail. I checked this morning and found that I have over 300
unread in the LBNA group. It seems I missed some opportunities to be on some
snail mail lists. : (

Tonight, I think I am feeling well enough to make some more stamps for my
pending trip to No. Cal. and Oregon! I have about 9 carved already for a series
I plan to place along the Umpqua river in Oregon. That particular area (around
Crater Lake) is a bit thin with only two boxes.

I am also planning to put some around the Lava Beds National Monument. Some of
the place names there just scream out for letterboxes: Skull Cave, Valentine
Cave, Captain Jack's Stronghold...
