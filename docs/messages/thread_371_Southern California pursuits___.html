Hi,

Just an Pacific Northwesterner traveling through the San Diego
region for a few days and wanted to post a note about boxes we'd
recently found:

We found the Joachin Murieta Outlaw, Tule Elk and San Juan
Capistrano Letterboxes all alive & well on 16 Feb 05.

Today, we unsuccessfully attempted the Solana Beach Stingray but did
manage to find San Diego Sails, Gliderport, Tea Time & La Jolla
Letterboxes all very much alive & well with many visitors. Not sure
where we went wrong with the Stingray but since we'll be in the area
a few more days we may reattempt it with any helpful hints folks
might be able to provide us. The boxes all provided us with such an
incredible vacation as the clues were great, the stamps incredible
and the vistas simply breathtaking!!! We took with us a Newbie
today to whom we were introducing this great hobby and she was
thoroughly impressed with the experience. Perhaps we'll have a new
convert in our midst??? Oh, we must've just passed by another
letterboxer at the Tea Time box--this individual was visiting from
Tampa, FL--how neat would that have been to exchange signature
stamps along the trail between visitors who both hail from such
distant places???

Thanks for some great boxes. We'll be in the area a few more days
and will attempt a couple more along the way. If anybody is
interested in linking up for a quick exchange, drop us a line and we
can try to link up somewhere. We're staying on North Island but
plan to drive all around the area for various treks.

Take care,

dvn2r ckr

dvn2r ckr,

It's great to have such illustrious LBers down in SoCal. Too bad your
visit coincides with these torrential rains; this is definitely not
the norm for us. I've already confirmed that one of my Pasadena
letterboxes was literally washed away in a flood, and am none to sure
about the status of a few others. Being from the Northwest, perhaps
you're use to this kind of weather.

Here's a little hint for the Solana Beach Stingray: the clues
say "return to the restrooms"; remember, this box was planted by
HummerLady, so this should clue you in on where at the restrooms to
start your search.

When planning your next trip down to SoCal, keep in mind that there
are 50 letterboxes hidden within a few squares miles around the
Pasadena area (it's like "Little Connecticut" around here), and 150
in Los Angeles County.
