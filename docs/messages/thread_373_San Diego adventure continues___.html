San Diego adventure continues...

From: dvn2rckr (dvn2rckr@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-02-21 06:15:59 UTC

Hey, again we braved the tornadoes, deluges and flooding and
attempted a few more San Diego letterboxes today. With a bit of
clarification for the Solana Stingray--we returned, experienced
the 'a-ha' moment but still no success in finding the box. It's
either buried somewhere in a ton of debris or floating somewhere in
the Pacific.

We then headed out to Batiquitos Lagoon and found that box with a
little bit of 'alternative' hunting. Somehow we found the box some
15 paces (30 steps) away from where the clues suggest. How we found
it? No clue! Just saw one of those 'letterbox/geocache stick/bark
altars' beneath a bush and the curiousity got the best of us. Lo
and behold, it turned out to be the very letterbox turned geocache
we were seeking. Yes, we're known for tenacity (ask Ryan aka Green
Tortuga about that) but we had just about given up on this box
altogether when I decided to investigate the altar. Perhaps the
clues should be modified to read "from the cactus, the letterbox is
hidden 3 feet due South beneath a shrub and covered with bark"
instead of its current "from the cactus, take 6 adult paces at a
150* heading. Then turn to 80* and take 9 adult paces. The
letterbox is hidden under the shrub on your right beneath a covering
of bark..." I have a feeling that the evolution of this letterbox
into a geocache has caused this box's migration? ;) Fortunately,
the letterbox turned hybrid geocache was still alive and well with
many visitors (mostly cachers) and it still contains its original
stamp.

Then we headed off to UCSD for the series there. Found the first
two in waning light with no difficulty. What an incredible
campus!!! That library was simply awesome!!! Then we attempted the
Sun God in virtual light-out conditions and my halogen flashlight
failed to help us find this box. :( We explored every nook and
cranny without success. It was an interesting spot, though. The
snails had taken up residence among the same roots/vines the clues
suggest the microbox to be in--it was sort of weird feeling around
snails in the dark. Fear Factor tryouts, perhaps??? Nonetheless,
we enjoyed our visit immensely as it's such a beautiful campus and
we even have relatives who went to med school there--now we have a
better feel for the many years of their lives they spent in and
around that area.

After our LB trek, we explored the library a bit more hoping to get
a glimpse of the original Dr Seuss manuscripts. Too bad it was
after hours--now we have an excuse to return!!! Linguistics played
a major part in half of my undergrad degree so it was so cool to
actually stumble upon such a treasure trove involving Theodor Geisel
himself!!! Thanks for another wave of great San Diego boxes. We
have a few more boxes in our sights for the couple remaining days we
have in the area--hopefully, the flash flood warnings and the other
weather related restrictions won't prevent us from checking them out.


What a cool city San Diego had proven to be!!! I feel like we've
only scratched the surface of this town...

Thanks again,

dvn2r ckr
