SoCal will be glad to see us go...

From: dvn2rckr (dvn2rckr@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-02-24 07:20:10 UTC

Well, tomorrow morn' we depart for our return trek home. I'm sure
most of you will be happy to see us go as it will likely be sunny
once we depart (that's sort of how it goes in the life of a
letterboxer-errant) and your inboxes won't fill up all that much in
our absence! ;) We tried and tried today to find a nice spot in
which to hide a long, lost orphaned letterbox in an effort to leave
a treat for all the folks who planted such neat letterboxes for us
to seek out around here but, alas, it was to no avail. We explored
Cabrillo Monument, the lighthouses, the tide pools and eventually
Sunset Cliffs and Dog Beach but couldn't find a spot anywhere that
was 'legal' and would conceal a completely orphaned letterbox as it
isn't very likely we'll get back here all that often to check on
it. (As it took 30+ years to get here the first time!!!) We'll
keep trying to find a nice spot in which to 'share' during our long
& hopefully sunny drive home. We found a bunch of spots around town
just screaming for urban type boxes but we just don't have the
resources for planting those types of boxes on this particular trip
and aren't mentally ready for the anguishing 'upkeep' that occurs in
absentia.

Anyways, thanks to all the great letterbox planters (& a couple of
geoca$he planters, too) down here. We'll attempt a few more on our
drive home as we're really close to achieving a personal milestone
and we don't have all that many opportunities back home to aid in
our quest. Hopefully, we'll find a nice, safe place for our little
box along the way home.

Thanks again for a great letterboxing tour of this region!!!

dvn2r ckr, rnrB & trkr
Steilacoom, WA
