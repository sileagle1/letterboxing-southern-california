> also still wondering are there letterboxes in Disneyland (CA)?

I am a little late getting in on this thread but here goes...

I have tried to plant a few empty micro boxes in different places
throughout Disneyland and California Adventure. I was putting them in
places that I thought for sure would remain hidden such as velcroing
them underneath the other side of an overhanging railing but most were
gone within a week. I had one that lasted over a month but when I
checked a couple of weeks ago, it was gone.

After my experiment with empty microboxes, it is my opinion that it is
darn near impossible to plant boxes inside Disneyland or DCA. You
might be able to get away with one somewhere around the perimeter of
the resort but I wouldn't waste the stamp material or ink trying to
put one inside.




Re: trying to post 2 new boxes

From: reddgroom (ReddGroom@aol.com) | Date: 2005-04-08 19:18:44 UTC

Eric,
#1 An empty microbox is a geocache, not a letterbox. If it doesn't
have a stamp, why call it a letterbox?
#2 You shouldn't be trying to place boxes (eiter geocache OR
letterboxes) on private property without the owners permission.
Disneyland in particular is cleaned & maintained so thoroughly
everyday, monitored with video cameras so heavily, and probably a
pretty major security & terrorist threat, what made you think you would
get away with planting a box/cache there anyway?

Thanks for trying to create an experience for all of us, but please
don't do anything further on someone else's property without
permission. Both geocaching & letterboxing are under fire right now in
some states as some people see us as littering, trespassing & trampling
the wilderness. Please don't do anything further to destroy our law-
abiding, family-friendly reputation.

Libby

agree about private property, and one should gain permission prior

someone else's property is too far reaching. When someone plants an
urban letterbox do you think that a city council or mayor are
consulted? The box is planted on someone else's property- city

anything other than he wouldn't waste the stamp material to put one
inside Disneyland, and that the only area that a box might work
would be around the perimeter. (I took that to mean "outside the
park", so I guess the city of Anaheim would be the one to gain
permission from).
