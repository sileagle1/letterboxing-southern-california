Thanks for your reply to my message. I am aware of what a letterbox is
(notice who is the owner of this group) and I am aware of what a
geocache is (note that it is not an empty container but one which contains
items for trade).

I have to echo what Don said about most letterboxes being placed without
permission. By leaving a letterbox somewhere, we are committing an act of
property abandonment (defined as abandoning, discarding, or otherwise
leaving any personal property). While one could argue the semantics (i.e.
the box isn't abandoned because people will be coming to it), we do leave a
box somewhere without any intention of ever picking that box up again (with
some notable exceptions of temporary letterboxes to commemorate events,
gatherings, etc.). As such, the mere act of placing a letterbox means that
we are all breaking the law unless we receive the express permission of
whatever enitity or individual owns, controls or oversees the land. There
is not a square inch of land that is not under the direct control of some
entity that we would need to get permission from with the exception of your
own property.

That being said, I do excercise caution when planting my boxes and try to
avoid places that finding a suspicous container would cause a panic (i.e.,
no airport or mall letterboxes). The experiment at the Disney parks was
done merely to see if it was even remotely possible to find other hiding
places and was done with an intentionally innocuous container that is
readily found every day in the parks that would cause no panic if found.

I do not and will not do anything to destroy the (semi-)law-abiding (see
property abandonment above), family friendly reputation of letterboxing as
this is a hobby I enjoy and I love to see it growing.
