From: alarch2 (alarch2@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-05-12 19:19:42 UTC
I retired my Marvys from letterboxing after seeing what happens to
water-soluble stamps in wet logbooks! I love brush pens, but it's been
hard to find a waterproof substitute. Pens are a bit tricky, too. You
can get streaks inking large areas, and if you use several on one
stamp the first ones can dry while you're adding the others. I also
bought one of the "option" pads with the 12 lift-out sections. It's
awkward compared to pens, but the colors are cool and the coverage is
pretty consistant. It was abt. $18.00. Call around, or check the web,
because it comes in different color themes- at that price, you should
have a choice! :)
-Alarch

From: rscarpen (letterboxing@atlasquest.com) | Date: 2005-05-13 16:33:27 UTC
> They may have been the wrong type, but they did not properly
> cover like ink pads do, so I returned them.

When you do your stamping with markers, the first ink you apply will
often dry before you finish coloring the rest of the stamp, so be sure
to breathe heavily on it just before stamping. It helps to "moisten"
the stamp and can improve your results dramatically! =)

I've also noticed that markers don't tend to work as well on large,
blocky areas where you might end up with streaks or else the ink
congels into little dots. (Not sure what to call that effect!)

So don't be fooled--markers aren't perfect for everything, but they do
have their uses! =)

-- Ryan
