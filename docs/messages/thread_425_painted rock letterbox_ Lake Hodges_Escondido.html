Re: painted rock letterbox: Lake Hodges/Escondido

From: Cattail (MLK) (marcikoski@earthlink.net) | Date: 2005-06-06 21:21:47 UTC
Hi - I've responded to CarolRae in a private email, but I thought
I'd provide some hints on this box for anyone interested. I'm
pretty sure the box has been moved since Don and Gwen visited four
years ago (see below).

Ok, so this is a really great trail. There's a message in the
letterbox basement archives about how the box has been moved from
its original location (which has been swamped with poison ivy), but
I can't seem to find it right now. When I went to find this box
last month, I found the original spot (which was pretty easy) but
then had a challenging time locating this box at its new spot -
the "revised" clues simply said it's 15 paces down the trail,
underneath a bush between two rocks.

So here's some clarification on that - go 10-15 paces down the trail
(away from the waterfall, downhill), and the box will be on your
right-hand side. The bush/shrub that you're looking for is quite
clear - there are two large-melon sized rocks right underneath it
(touching each other). You won't have to move aside any shrubbery
to see these rocks - they're quite visible from the trail. When I
found the box, it was simply perched on top of these rocks out in
the open - I rehid the box just behind the two rocks, and piled a
couple of small rocks on top of it so that it was better concealed.

I hope this helps - it's a great little trail, and I'm thankful that
someone took the time to rehabilitate this box and move it to a
better location.

Cattail :)

Re: painted rock letterbox: Lake Hodges/Escondido

From: alarch2 (alarch2@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-06-17 20:19:02 UTC
Hi everyone-
I found this box this week! The log has entries going back to Jan. 1,
2001-very interesting to see! Sadly, at some point it was wet, so not
all the stamps are legible, but still a great letterboxing history
document.
Once I arrived at the waterfall, I ignored the original clues and used
the ones Cattail gave, which were extremely helpful, especially the
part about not having to move any shrubbery to see the rocks. =)
There's some confusion about the paces, though. From the box back to
the waterfall I took large steps and it was still 30 paces (60
footfalls) for me. Would've been more had I been walking normally.
Maybe 15 paces was the distance from the original to the new hiding place?
The big mystery is, who rescued this box and left it sitting on the
rocks where Cattail found it?
-Alarch
