Hi Dan

Usually I use the soft lead pencil tranfer technique, but found on
really intricate images the acetone transfer method works best. FYI
not all nail polish has acetone. Anyway after obtaining the image I
want I print it on our HP printer/scanner. I then scour the stamp
material(PZKut original A grade, don't know if it works on the new
orange stuff)with a wetted with acetone paper towel. This changes the
stamp surface slightly and makes it just a tad gummy. I then place the
copy face down and rub it like a pencil transfer. I usually go over
the transfered image with a ballpoint to make sure that it doesn't
blurr.
The Painted Cave stamp wasn't done with any of those techniques, but
it was a direct draw on the material and carve. It was simple enough
that I could get away with that technique.
I use acetone to clean up stamps and also prepare the back surface for
glue, and you can get it in 1 qt cans at you local hardware store.

Don
