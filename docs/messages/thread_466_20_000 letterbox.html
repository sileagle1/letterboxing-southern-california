Thanks, Don. Most of my early box finds were one's you and Gwen did
(and two from Chowder). I was inspired to get creative. After months
of thinking about it, I finally put out some boxes of my own, then
became a member of LbNA.org and posted them. I was surprised and
delighted by the immediate response.

After posting the first three, I realized I'd forgotten that I wanted
to put the stamp and it's name on the first page of the logbook, as
was common in letterboxes I'd found. But when I went back to them,
all three had already been found, and one had six visitors in the
first 48 hours after I posted the clues.

So I went back and stamped the other three before I posted their
clues, which is when I noticed the 20,000 landmark. I then recieved a
few emails about it, and realized I'd better get on this discussion
group, since letterboxing was obviously a much more interactive and
vibrant culture then I'd realized.

This is my first message, and I think the first thing you'll all learn
about me is that I'm terribly long-winded. Brevity was never my
strong suit.

Thanks for the warm welcome.
Kelsung

p.s. Ignore the peacheseasy name. This is an old yahoo account I used
to use for a band I worked with, and it was quicker than signing up to
yahoo again.
