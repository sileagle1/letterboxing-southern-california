Semi-Mystery Bonus Box in Whittier & June Bug Hitchhiker

From: trentlaudes (trentlaudes@excite.com) | Date: 2003-09-15 22:15:26 UTC
My wife, French Hen, and I rolled out of bed early this morning and,
even though it was a work day, decided the weekend needed to be
extended. So I called in a vacation day and we hot-footed it to the
city of Whittier, where Jam & Jelly have recently re-planted
their "Semi-Mystery Box" along with a bonus box. I'd already found
the first plant, along with a bonus box with a stamp carved by
Jelly. How any civilian ever found the original box is beyond me;
seemed like such a good hiding place, but they do have extensive
upkeep at this park, which we saw ample evidence of early this
morning with all the leaf-blowers, and people wading in streams
fishing out stuff, and sprinklers on, etc. My wife, who is new to
the sport, read out the clues, and in short order found the mystery
box and . . . A HITCHHIKER!! She was thrilled; it was the June Bug
HH started by Camp Fire Lady and planted in this box by Purple Iris.
Now on to the bonus box. Early this morning, the lawn sprinklers
were soaking the area of the bonus box, but, not to be daunted, I
zigged and zagged between the water jets, and found the bonus box
with a minimum of drenching (I don't think this qualifies for the LB
Purple Heart, but it was cold). Loved the location of this letterbox
J&J, but I'll bet that Jelly couldn't have planted it without being
on Jam's shoulders--French Hen and I are first stamp!! Thanks for a
memorable morning playing hooky from work.
--Buzzard P8 F60 (that's right, June Bug is my 60th)
