From: Lisa Correia (doomed_lenore@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-02-01 16:36:12 UTC-08:00
Hello all!

I am getting ready to place my first box and I have a few questions about stamping etiquette. I wanted to ask this group since I presume that each area has it's own "culture" when it comes to these things.

In my limited boxing experience (2 months now) I have stamped the last or next to last page in 3 different box journals. My husband and I each have our own stamp, and write our own comments, but we always fit them both on to one page so as not to waste space. Looking back through the journals I have noticed several occasions where it appears that a family of 4 or 5 have given each of their young children a page of their own to stamp. So one party takes up 5 pages in a journal. Sadly, most of the page is just blank space with only a stamp and first name. I'm seeing this as wasted paper and space since the stamps and signatures could easily fit on one page. This seems particularly sad when the placer went to the trouble of hand making the journal.

So here are my questions:

1. What is the etiquette for an unrelated party going back and using the other half of a page to sign in? (This would put two boxers on one page). This also goes for using the back side of a page if your sure the stamp won't "bleed" through.

2. Would it be offensive for the placer of a box to suggest in the clues that parties share a page when possible?

Any comments would be appreciated.

Lost Lenore

Hi Lost Lenore,
I have found many boxes where multiple parties stamp in on the same
page, usually after the pages have all been stamped once.
In your own box it seems perfectly appropriate to request that people
try to conserve space when stamping in, particularly if the log is
small. I think I've seen clues where the placer made a similar request.
Thanks to all for the advice re: the hounds. I'll have to sneak out
when my dog's not looking some day, to hunt for those boxes! I tried
last week, but he gave me those "eyes" :)
Happy Boxing!
-Alarch

From: Gwen and Don Jackson (foxsecurity@earthlink.net) | Date: 2006-02-01 19:44:54 UTC-08:00
Hi Lost
Here is my answer to your questions, but first I will tell you that Gwen & I carry extra logbooks just in case the box we find has a logbook problem. I think having every member of a family stamp in on multiple pages is a little self centered. Gwen and I try to keep to one page, maybe front and back,when we can. If you are placing your letterbox then set it up the way you wish. We have crammed our personal stamp next to others so I don't see the problem.

Don
