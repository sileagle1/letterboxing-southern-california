Eaton Canyon Hummingbird Letterbox Replaced.

From: buzzardpasadena (disbarro@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2006-03-27 01:20:59 UTC
For a second time, I've replaced the Eaton Canyon Hummingbird
letterbox. According to my letterbox stampbook, this box was
originally placed by TwoFamilies on 1/1/2003, and is my second found
box on 2/22/2003. It disappeared soon after, and will likely disappear
again since this is a very popular hiking destination. My carving is
based on the original stamp image. The stamp itself is not that great,
but you will love Eaton Falls, which was visited and written about by
John Muir in 1877.

I've also replaced the Eaton Canyon Letterbox, which I had removed for
first aid but hadn't previously replaced because of poison oak growth
at the site. The location of this box is very pretty, but is absolutely
surrounded by poison oak. I would never have put a box here myself, but
this is the original placement.
--Buzzard
