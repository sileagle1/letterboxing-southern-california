Heron and Tinman

From: Q (puppygirl13@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2006-06-05 22:29:48 UTC
Hi everybody! After taking about a year off from letterboxing, I
rejoined the sport by visiting the Prayer Rock box in Vermont, a nice
collection of boxes in New York City and Connecticut, and the Heron
and Tinman box in Long Beach.

I didn't think there would be a lot of visitors to the Heron and
Tinman since you need a boat to access this box, but I was surprised
by how few people had logged in in the five years this box has been
there. My boyfriend and I rented a kayak, paddled until our arms were
sore, got soaked from head to toe (letterboxing gear stayed dry in a
special bag that came with the kayak) and generally had a blast. The
only thing I wish we had brought was my dog - we didn't know she would
be allowed, but we will return later in the summer to do it again with
her.

Anyway, I highly recommend this letterbox. And just thought I'd say
hey :) -Q

Re: Heron and Tinman

From: troublew_tracy (troublew_tracy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-07-11 23:20:06 UTC
Well, I got out and found the box this past weekend, so I can answer my
own question: no chance of seasickness here. ;) It was certainly a fun
time (and a good workout!) My sister and I shared a kayak and her
boyfriend had one to himself. He coasted along pretty quickly and kept
having to stop and wait for us to catch up, but we enjoyed it, slow and
steady as we were. ;) Like Q, I definitely recommend it-- it was a
challenge without being too physically difficult and a neat place;
there were tons of kite surfers nearby and I'd love to go back some
time to visit the area with the jellyfish that you can touch (they
don't sting). I had the directions to "I'm a Heron Two" along with me
as well, but we were pretty worn out just going after the one box and
it seemed a bit challenging to try to figure out where it was from the
water rather than with the more detailed by land directions.

A couple of little tips for anyone who's as clueless as myself: First,
wear open-toed shoes. It's that or barefoot only allowed on the kayaks
and you will want something protecting your feet when you retrieve the
box. This is probably obvious, but I was in letterboxing mode, which
meant tennis shoes, so I had to borrow my sister's sandals when we got
there. Also, the place we rented the kayaks from was quite reasonable
(in our opinions anyway), but it was just a little stand on the beach,
so it was cash only, so once again I was unprepared. My sister's
boyfriend payed, but I made up for it by treating them both to a late
lunch/early dinner at Ruby's. :)

Tdyans
