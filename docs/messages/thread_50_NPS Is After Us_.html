NPS Is After Us!

From: Gnosis 4-H Club (gnosis4-h@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2003-09-19 00:25:12 UTC-07:00
I got one of those emails you never hope to see - a law enforcement person
from the National Park Service informing us to remove our boxes (they just
mentioned the Peter Strauss series thus far) immediately. It went on to say
how the NPS has policies forbidding letterboxing and geocaching in their
parks.

I'm not going to raise a stink, partly out of paranoia about a law
enforcement person with the word "federal" in their title, and partly
because 4H is also a federal program so I don't want to get the whole
organization into trouble over this. But, since I know we've found boxes
planted by others in the greater LA area which are also on federal park
lands, I felt I should mention it here as a warning. The email didn't say if
this was the start of some crackdown by the NPS, or if they just happened to
find our series by mistake. I'm hoping they don't intend to look at the
letterboxing.org lists and start emailing everyone who's planted in their
parks, though it would be fairly easy for them to do if they felt so
inspired.

Annette
Gnosis 4-H Club

Re: [letterboxing-socal] NPS Is After Us!

From: Gwen and Don Jackson (foxsecurity@earthlink.net) | Date: 2003-09-22 09:56:31 UTC-07:00
For further explanation on "C" count, "C" is for confiscation of letterboxes from our land that the NPS deem inappropriate in "their" rules. There are letterboxes on land controlled by NPS, and if the clues are properly masked ,or are not posted but passed on from letterboxer to letterboxer they survive. If you use the location in the title or text the letterbox will not last long. If you set up the letterbox as a mystery box it will probably last. We have atleast one NPS employee that gets the main chat list, and I believe the chat list is checked on a regular basis. My guess is that this list is NPS free ,but don't bet that it will stay that way. Be cautious in posting anything about letterboxes , or clues that relate to letterboxes on land controlled by NPS on this ,or any other list. I have seen entries on letterbox logs in BLM areas that have a notation by a NPS employee that they were "Noting"the location. Am I paranoid, probably, but I'm planning on not having our "C" count increased,as their rules don't seem to match our rules. Don
