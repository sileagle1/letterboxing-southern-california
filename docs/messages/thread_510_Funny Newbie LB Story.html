Funny Newbie LB Story

From: Sheri (sherit@csufresno.edu) | Date: 2006-09-10 02:00:07 UTC
We were searching for a box near Morro Bay (sorry I can't remember the
name at the moment). Anyway, being new we were not prepared with all
the gadgets that letterboxers need to properly find a box. We were
kind of "winging it". So the clues were pretty easy and we got to the
land mark in the clue. Then it said to turn 70% and look for an oak
tree with a moss covered rock at the base. One of the items we didn't
have was a compass. So using my Junior High geometry skills I
estimated 70% and looked around for a tree with a moss covered rock.
Too my excitement, I found one and hidden beneath it, a container.
When I opened it, it contained and assortment of trinkets. Turns out
it was a geocache, not the box I was looking for at all. So, I signed
in the book and told how I found it. Then I did what any loyal
letterboxer does and stamped it! For the sake of the game I also took
a trinket and left one. What did I take? A small compass! Then I went
back to the land mark and found my letterbox!

Mic n Goof

Excellent work! You did exactly the right thing. And now you have a
compass, too :-D Having stamped the logbook, perhaps later finders
will see it give LB a look-see. Lots of LBers were (or still are,
like us) geocachers, and many geocachers also letterbox: we do!

Our first letterbox was found while looking for a geocache :-) We
didn't have a stamp, of course, but I noted in the log the basic info,
and then looked for the LBNA to report things. Unfortunately, the
owner of the LB never responded to us, nor to the owner of the
geocache, who moved the cache a little to prevent confusion, despite
that the geocache had been there for a year or so before the LB. The
LB listing actually was pulled a few months later, which is a shame :-(

Anyway, congratulations on your (first?) geocache find. If you
haven't signed up yet, you should, so that you can log your find.
Backdating logs is very easy on the geocaching.com site. A lot of
geocachers will get a kick out of your story :-)
