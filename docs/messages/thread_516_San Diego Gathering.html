San Diego Gathering

From: geishagirl29127 (geishagirl29127@hotmail.com) | Date: 2007-01-14 17:48:12 UTC
Hello Everyone!

OK, here are some details (finally!) for the San Diego gathering.

It is planned for Saturday, 3/24, from 11AM-3PM in San Diego's
Presidio Park, which is right behind Old Town. Details on the actual
location within the park will be announced as we get closer to the
date.

Some of the activities I plan on having include:

* Lunch - Do you think it would be a fun idea to have a
little "potluck?" Or should we have bring your own lunch?

* Carving Class and Letterboxing 101 - This will be a little session
that will be presented by one of our seasoned letterboxers. This
class will probably be held after lunch around 1PM.

* Make-Your-Own Memento - There will be a little arts-and-crafts
souvenier that you will be able to make and take home with you to
remember the event by.

* Event Series - Aside from the Event Stamp, which Don from Don-and-
Gwen has so graciously offered to carve, there will be a LB series
planted in the park for the event!

* MAKE NEW FRIENDS AND HAVE LOTS OF FUN!!

After the class, the afternoon will be pretty much open for folks to
do as they choose. I'm sure there will be lots of letterboxing to do!

Folks, please send me your thoughts and comments to
geishagirl29127@hotmail.com. I would really appreciate any feedback
that you may have. Also, if possible, please RSVP by 3/16 so I can
get an idea for how many people will be there. I will post this on
AtlasQuest, so if you could RSVP there, I'd really appreciate it!

Looking forward to meeting a bunch of you and having a great time!

D&G,
We all know your son-in-law carves the most beautiful letterboxing
stamps in the realm. Your own stamps are second to none (except for,
of course, your son-in-law's). By the way, I have a Word-of-Mouth
(WOM) letterbox that has been hidden for a couple of years in the San
Gabriel Mountains above my house in Pasadena. I hid it, but never
gave anyone the clues. It's a nice carving, and I have every reason
to believe it's still lying up there, undisturbed beneath the oaks.
The location can be reached by a 6-mile round-trip "moderate" hike in
the Angeles National Forest. Please note that this is a "moderate"
hike, not an "easy" hike. Hiking time will be at least 3 hours, and
you will get winded. It's also wilderness-y, and it's possible
you'll run into some lions and tigers and bears--I'm serious. I will
give the clues to anyone who attended the 2nd California LB Gathering
at San Luis Obispo on 10/04/03 (those googlers who carved such
creative stamps, like Sir B., Jugglermouse, and Ash Raven [love the
coke bottle stamp]); anyone who attended the Kernville Gathering,
4/24/04; anyone who attended the Old Zoo Gathering on 5/22/05; or
anyone who attended the little informal SoCal Gathering at the Santa
Ana Botanical Garden in the rain on 4/17/04. I know who you are, so
if you're interested in this clue, identify yourself and Email me at
disbarro@sbcglobal.net.
--Buzzard

From: gwendontoo (foxsecurity@earthlink.net) | Date: 2007-03-27 00:44:58 UTC
We just got back to Kernville this afternoon and wanted to post our
thanks to Geishagirl. Putting on an event takes a bunch of work and
GG's efforts really paid off. We think it was a huge success.

Now for those that missed the carving class you also missed a really
focused instructor. I was trying to hear the questions with my one
semi good ear,and remain focused on the stamp in front of me..

Now I've never tried carving in bright sunlight and my large
magnifier was being swiveled about letting folks try it out. Suddenly
I raised up and declared "I smell smoke!" Sure enough I had started a
smoldering piece of paper right in front of me. Luckily no one had a
container of water handy.

Tip #34 Do not use magnifiers in sunlight.

Sunday had us finding the 6 letterboxes in Tecolote Park. Also we
bumped into two different groups of letterboxers on the trail. We
scored two more exchanges.

Don
