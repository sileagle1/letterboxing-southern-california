Catalina Report

From: troublew_tracy (troublew_tracy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-11-05 17:44:40 UTC
Well, the Catalina trip was a bit of a bust as far as letterboxing
goes. I went there with five sets of clues and of those was only
able to find one (The Island of Romance...). This is not because
the others weren't there to find-- I don't know if they were or
not. But we discovered when we arrived that the road that you need
to take to get to three of the boxes has been declared dangerous by
the county due to rockslides, so it was not accessible to hikers,
bikes or golf carts, so there was basically no way for us to even
get close to those letterboxes. That left one more, for which we
had trouble deciphering the clue. It seemed to be written pretty
straightforwardly, but it had us looking for a road by a name that
no road on the island seemed to have. By that point we were tired
enough and disappointed enough that we decided to just let that one
go. Someday when the road is re-opened, we'll have to go back and
find those three and figure out the one.

On the bright side, I did plant one letterbox of my own called
Birthday Bison. I'll be adding it to LBNA and AQ soon. And despite
our letterboxing disappointments, we still had a lot of fun on the
island. We went on an hour-plus horseback ride, walked up to the
Wrigley Memorial and back (that really wore us out), walked around
town and looked at shops and enjoyed some good food. Oh, and we
didn't know until we got there, but the triathlon was yesterday, so
it was a bit crowded in town, but it was neat to see a bit of the
race in the morning.
