Ok, Tdyans, if your goal was to driving me insane it is working! I
have been staring at your Get LOST clue for a couple of weeks and have
been searching cryptology websites to no avail. Don't suppose you want
to give a poor girl a hint?

Anyway, I have another question for everyone out there. Yesterday I
went to the 99 cent store (didn't find any lock n/& locks, but some
other things) and I made myself a first aid kit to take with me when
boxing. I have found damaged boxes and full logbooks and wanted to be
able do something. So, if I find a full logbook and replace it, what
should I do? Leave both logbooks and contact the owner? Or take the
full logbook, contact the owner and mail it to them? Also, just in
case anyone is interested, the little logbooks I got are miniature
(3"x3") scrapbooks... only 13 pages, but really good paper. I figure
they are good for replacements until someone can get to their box and
do something themselves.

Thanks for your ideas,

Re: Going crazy & replacing logbooks

From: troublew_tracy (troublew_tracy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-11-22 21:24:33 UTC
Muahahaha!

I'm delighted to hear both that you're finding it a challenge and
that you're still interested in figuring it out anyway. The only
hint I will give is that the directions to the box are shorter than
you may think. Other than that, all I can say is that Dr. Waxler has
already given you the key....

As for replacing logbooks, I think it would be best, if it will all
fit, to add the new book but leave the old one, then contact the
placer. If there's not a lot of room, you could also just add a few
index cards-- enough for a few finders after you to stamp in if it
takes the placer a little while to replace the book. Most people
would probably appreciate you pulling a full book and mailing it to
them, but some might not, so I think the best policy would be to
always ask before pulling.

Tdyans
