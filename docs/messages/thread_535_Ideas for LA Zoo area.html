From: Suzanne (realroadrunner@earthlink.net) | Date: 2006-12-02 15:17:24 UTC
Hi:

Last weekend the Sonoran and I did Q's Idiot Box Series (got 3 of the six boxes, but that's
becxause our companion gave out) and Buzzard's Old Zoo Gathering Series "Amir's Garden
1 & 2". Combined these two hunts make a good long hike, starting at Q's picking up
Buzzard's and returning via Q's. We only found Amir's Garden #2, but I'd vote for it as one
of the best boxes in L.A. (carving and location). And Q's boxes never disappoint.

I definately second the idea of going for Amir's Garden #2! Be warned -- the hike up is
short, but pretty steep. So if your one-mile limit is for time, no problem. But if it is for
mobility or "in-shape" issues, be warned! (It is still worth it! Just take it slow....)

Also, check out LbNA -- I was looking up some boxes a few weeks ago that were near the
Old Zoo Park, and there was one called the Lost Texan that looked interesting. It might be
more of a drive-by -- I don't know for sure because we ended up going after different
boxes.

Also, Q's Batcave series (of which one, I think, is missing?) is somewhere in Griffith Park.
But I don't know how close that is to the Zoo.

(Speaking of the Zoo, I have been toying with the idea of hiding a condor stamp in the zoo,
somewhere near the usually deserted Condor display. But then again, hiding it on Zoo
property might be a very, very, very bad idea. Anyone with more planting experience have
an opinion?)

- Sitzmak

Katherine,
You might also try our ...

From: piper_paws (hgraziano@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2006-12-02 18:25:59 UTC
We found "The Lost Texan" and "Griffith Park: Carousel" at the end of
October. Both were there alive and well at that time. The Lost Texan
is an easy walk and it is cool to see the Old Zoo and the Carousel is
also an easy walk. When we were there, there was no log book for the

and really good! (Hope it is still there!)
