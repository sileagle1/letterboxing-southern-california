Re: Submit Your Patch Designs

From: troublew_tracy (troublew_tracy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-01-31 04:34:34 UTC
The only rule right now is that it needs to be in jpg format. When
a design is chosen and submitted to the company that makes the
patches, we may find that a few changes need to be made, but that's
something to worry about down the road-- for now, submit whatever
your heart desires. Obviously, not too huge of a file is good, too--
we don't want to bog down the page that Mark and Sue give us (or
bog down their inbox for that matter). A high resolution file might
be nice to have on hand for when we submit the design, but for now
it just needs to be good enough for everyone to see and get an idea
of what it looks like to vote on.

I will point out that the company that manufactures the patches lets
you have up to 7 colors. After that, you pay an extra 10 cents for
each extra color. They also charge more based on how much
embroidery is involved, so if the patch would be almost entirely
embroidery (rather than having the background material visible),
then it would be more expensive. And the other factor in price will
be the size, which we can always adjust, unless the design is super
intricate.
