From: gwendontoo (foxsecurity@earthlink.net) | Date: 2007-02-11 18:05:21 UTC
Hi Kwistie
You should remember that Letterboxes have an artsy side and with most
art folks have different tastes.
Welcome to the reactions placers sometime receive. Once in a while
several of us that have a bunch of plants compare 'Contact the Finder'
notes we have received. There have been some real doozies.
Here is one we received on a rest stop driveby a few years back.

"Couldn't find your letterbox. You should change the clues. Is
the 'Interpretive' signs the ones that have information on them?(Duh!
Don's comment)
What do Junipers look like? (The only trees around! Don's comment)
It was cold and rainy out so you should make the walk shorter."

Hmmm, maybe we should have left overcoats and umbrellas in the parking
lot too!

For any negative comments you might receive you will probably get ten
fold in positive comments.

Funhog had a pretty good one and it went something like this.

"Couldn't find your crummy letterbox. It cost me 5 bucks to park and
your clues suck."
At least it was succinct and to the point.

For those that have found or attempted the Hog's letterboxes you always
have to hike a little further than you think, her clues are rather
obscure, but the end is always a treat with pretty good stamps.

Keep on a hiding them! We all get some classic notes.

Don

From: gwendontoo (foxsecurity@earthlink.net) | Date: 2007-02-11 18:41:08 UTC
This brings up an interesting point that some folks haven't thought
much about and that is the rating systems on AQ with Blue Diamonds and
Placers favorite. We (Gwen and I) have opted out of the system and do
not set our letterboxes up as favorites or have them marked with BDs.
We do not rate others letterboxes either. We feel that rating tends to
detract from the experience, but that is just our personal choice.
So when registering your finds at AQ on our letterboxes you don't have
to rate any of our letterboxes as the rating won't show up.

We are not being critical of anyone that chooses to use the system as
that choice is totally up to the individual placer.

Don

Boy, do I resemble *that* remark! No talent at carving, that's me :-o
You haven't seen a bad carving until you see our personal stamp in a
log. And I actually cleaned it up some after buying some tools with
narrower blades. Heaven knows how folks will react to our first LB
when we hide it, as the stamp will probably be a disaster, but I
refuse to buy a stamp at the store. Which is kind of funny, as I am a
rubber stamper of many years, but this is different. This is one of
the few "Traditional American Rules" that I want to adhere to. Some
of the "rules", such as not responding to e-mails from finders or
seekers, and having no obligation to maintain a hide (the
environmentalist in me rebels at the idea of letterbox litter from
trashed hides), I find idiotic, but the idea of hand-carving every
stamp appeals to me, despite my complete lack of anything like
artistic ability in this area. I'll be the last person to ever
criticise someone for their stamp. I might write to ask what it is,
if I can't figure it out, but I won't call it "bad".

As for jerks, I just try to ignore them, especially as the law won't
let me shoot them ;-)
