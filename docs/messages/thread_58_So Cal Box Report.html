So Cal Box Report

From: Gnosis 4-H Club (gnosis4-h@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2003-10-09 10:38:49 UTC-07:00
While in San Diego yesterday, we managed to find time to do a little box
hunting.

We tried to find the Santee Lakes box, but just could not decipher the third
line of the clue well enough. After checking every tree near the playground
and asking a park ranger for his thoughts on the clue, we gave up. We did
get a fish to eat one of our worms while fishing though :)

Then we headed to Escondido and the Jack Creek Falls box. Don and Gwen, we
are happy to say it is still in great shape and hidden well. And we noticed
no Buzzard stamp, which set my boxing partner, Nik, into a happy fit of
giggles - we've gotten very used to seeing Buzzard's stamp in every box we
find. It was a great spot to visit and we ended up staying at the park until
well after closing - we got kicked out by park rangers eventually :) The
clue page should probably be changed though to reflect the correct closing
time of the park, which is 6:30pm during fall and winter and 7:30pm during
spring and summer.

Annette
Gnosis 4-H Club

Annette,

I got a kick out of seeing my name unexpectantly mentioned in a
letterboxing post, thank you. You'll be happy to know I've only
found one San Diego County stamp: the Batiquitos Lagoon stamp in
Carlsbad, my hometown. Gnosis certainly have been busy little
letterbox-placing bees, and I am first stamp in your first 10
letterboxes (I've even got some certificates to prove it). I showed
up at the Los Angeles County Fair 4-H booth to visit you all, but you
were not at the fair that day. It sucks about your Peter Straus
Ranch boxes being recalled, but that's part of the game. You missed
the BEST time at the San Luis Obispo letterbox gathering last weekend
(at least I think you missed it; there were so many letterboxers
there I didn't have a chance to meet half of them). Thanks for
planting so many boxes, and I hope you haven't been soured on your
efforts by certain governmental agencies.
Buzzard P9 F82 X15 E2
