Gathering Thanks and Trip Report

From: Wisconsin Hiker (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2007-04-01 14:36:20 UTC
I'm finally getting around to filing a brief trip report from last
weekend. I've been working in California since mid-February on a
project that will last until the end of April. Some weekends I work,
some I head home, and for others Martini Man comes out here so we can
do some letterboxing. When I found out that a gathering was planned
in the San Diego area for the weekend of March 24, we figured that
would be a good weekend for some boxing and a chance to meet some
local letterboxers.

Martini Man flew out Thursday night and after I attended a meeting on
Friday morning we were off to Crystal Cove State Park. We found a
box there and also planted one (clues were posted on LBNA
yesterday). We liked hiking the hilly trails there and enjoyed the
ocean views! After that we scrambled along the rocks, with the waves
crashing nearby, to find the Tidepool Lighthouse box lots of fun.
Then it was a stop in the BUSY town of San Juan Capistrano. It
seemed they were having a big celebration that weekend (the return of
the swallows?) and people were all over the place. However we did
manage to find the very nicely carved Ghosts of Capistrano series.
Then we were back on the trails to find the Friendly Ghost box (and
the Sandpiper hitchhiker!) before we called it a day.

Saturday morning we headed to Rancho Bernardo to find three boxes
(Painted Rocks, Kumeyaay Indian, and Hodgee) and enjoyed this
pleasant area. We then marveled at the colorful Queen Califia's
Magical Circle before finding the Indians of the Acorn. After that
it was off to the gathering.

Geisha Girl met us with a nice goodie bag with carving media and
raffle ticket a nice touch and much appreciated! After getting our
name tags and chatting a bit, we headed over to meet some other
people. It turned out to be Don & Gwen, who we have corresponded
with a bit when planning a trip to California. We spent the next
hour or so chatting and exchanging with several other people,
including Lee & Nancy, Grumpy Grinch, Peter K. and others. We also
were able to purchase the cool new California letterboxing patches.
Martini Man especially enjoyed chatting with Acorn & Stafish, two
very cute little girls belonging to Punky and the King of Pinecrest.
We're thinking of heading to their neck of the woods next March. We
brought 6 cooties to the event which we managed to slip into various
packs and only came home with one new one ourselves (Song in your
Head) a good trade for us! We took a short jaunt up the nearby
hill to find two boxes, then came back for more socializing.
Wisconsin Hiker was one of the lucky raffle winners and the index
cards will be put to good use in making future box logbooks. Next we
were pleased to go out on the trail with Tdyans and Alarch (?). We
did the SD Sports Series, Creatures of Wonderland and Park Pleasures
series with them, juggling the various sets of clues to do them on
the same route along the stream and back. We met Kelsung, Zuhl is
Cool and Naught Zoot(?) along the trail as they were nearing their
milestone 100th find. Then we were back at the gathering spot where
we said our goodbyes to whoever was left and thanked Geisha Girl for
organizing such a nice event. We headed out with Pete & Wanda to
find the last series of boxes (Enchanted Forest) and even managed to
find the Longhorns box. Then it was back to the hotel after
an "eventful" day.

Sunday morning we headed to Iron Mountain. It started out quite
foggy, but gradually cleared as we made our way up to the Ramona
Overlook. By the time we got there it was a gorgeous day and view
and a great place to find Ramona Road Runner , our 2,100th box! We
then headed over to the summit and found both the Water Works and
Iron Mountain box (and the Mille Borne Hitchhiker). It was a busy
trail on a nice Sunday morning. We then walked along a short nature
trail to find Cozy Camp and Dos Picos Park and stopped to stamp into
Mostly Monkeys. We ended the day with another long hike at Volcan
Mountain where we managed to find the Apple Pie a la Mode series and
all 5 boxes in the Grumpy Grandeur series. Quite a bit of uphill on
this hike and some great views and interesting landmarks at the top!

Monday we only had time for a few boxes (and a lot of traffic!). The
weather wasn't as nice, but we enjoyed finding Diamond Valley Lake,
Maze Stone (eerie place in the fog), and Signal Races.

Thanks to all of you who place boxes in California we appreciate
the opportunity you give us to find new places and see some of the
local spots. Thanks also to all of you who made us feel so welcome
at the event. It was nice to meet some of the locals who we have
seen stamped into many logbooks!

Wisconsin Hiker & Martini Man

P.S. Martini Man has sent to notes to all the placers to let them
know the status of the various boxes we found.
