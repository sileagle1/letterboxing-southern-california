Don & Gwen,

We haven't advanced as far as considering dates as yet. At this
point, it's only in the dreaming stage.

At the SLO Event, 20 new letterboxes were placed in park just for
event day, then retrieved that evening after the end of the event. I
envisioned something similar for El Dorado, not only to provide a lot
of boxes for event-goers to find, but to make it more interesting for
those of us who have already found your boxes at this location
(actually, I'm still missing two of yours). I'd like to plant a
couple in the Nature Park, but wouldn't want to go against the
explicit wishes of the park manager, who apparently doesn't approve
of such things (going against the implicit wishes is a different
matter). This is a pretty big park, maybe there still are some good
hiding places left on the non-Nature Park side--Doughnut and I might
need to check that out. Do you know of another park in greater Los
Angeles similar in size, diversity and interest as El Dorado? EUREKA-
-Griffith Park seems like the logical choice!!
--Buzzard

From: trentlaudes (trentlaudes@excite.com) | Date: 2003-10-16 05:18:18 UTC
Doughnut,

Griffith Park is lovely. And every weekend is filled with families
enjoying the picnic tables and barbecues, and trails, and TravelTown,
and train rides, and Merry-Go-Rounds, etc. I think it's the largest
urban park in the U.S., though I may be mistaken. There are two
letterboxes already residing in the park: "Two Cats and a Troll"
(planted by Hamish Macbeth and Cousine Knueppe), which is located in
a beautiful fern dell, and "The Mount Hollywood Letterbox" (planted
by Mandrill), which requires a 2 mile hike to the top of Mount
Hollywood from its trailhead in the Park; this letterbox was
immortalized as part of the (in)famous Pandy "Trip of Doom" (don't
worry, Pandy didn't break her ankle here, that happened in Pasadena--
I could tell you a letterboxing yarn about this incident. Griffith
Park is so big and diverse, you could hide a hundred boxes here, let
loose forty letterboxers, and not see a single one during the day.

That said, the French Hen thought of another fine park located closer
to home (your and my home), and that is Brookside Park in Pasadena.
It's the home of the Rose Bowl, and an aquatic center, and a golf
course, and hiking trails, and a casting pond, etc. And it is in the
historic Arroyo Grande, and would probably be a wonderful place for a
gathering, though I'd want to check it out more completely, because
I've never really explored it by foot.
--Buzzard
