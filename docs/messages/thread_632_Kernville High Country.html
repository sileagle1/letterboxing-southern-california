From: gwendontoo (foxsecurity@earthlink.net) | Date: 2007-10-01 00:21:50 UTC
Gwen and I had a opportunity to do a bit of maintenance on
our "Anniversary/Western Divide Letterbox" yesterday as her cousins
from the UK were here celebrating their first anniversary, and we
decided what better spot to take them to.

The high country around Kernville is absolutely beautiful this time of
year and the leaves are just starting to turn. The temps were in the
low 60s for most of the day and while there were a few folks walking
about in the big trees, nary a soul could be found at our hidden
waterfall.

There have been a few stampins at the letterbox and it was really
surprising how many of them were celebrating their anniversary as well.
Celebrating an annivesary isn't really a requirement for this letterbox.

This LB is our first plant and while the location is pretty spectacular
the stamp is pretty so-so.

In a few months this box and others up there will be snowed in for the
winter so if anyone is planning on the trip after the OC Gathering on
October 13 make sure you check in here or check with the Forest Service
for road conditions.

All you folks have fun in Huntington Beach and even though we will miss
this one we will try to make sure our Seal Beach Town Tour is up to
speed and try to replace the logbook in Dogs Mandatory.

Don

Gwen and I did some maintenance on our High Country boxes. These are 3
season boxes and after the first snowfall most of the back country
roads get shut.
Right now and for the next few months is a perfect time to head up
there. Temps in Kernville are still at 90 degrees but up higher it is
20 degrees cooler. It is deer season for the next few weeks but after
then there are hardly any folks up there. By then there should be some
color changes as well.

(I've seen more deer on our property than in the mountains!)

The boxes we checked on are on the Western Divide Hwy going from
Kernville to Springville.

Don
