Happy New Year Everybody,
Just got an amazing story about one of my letterboxes. Had to share
it. My box is called Blast off and it's a cartoon rocket stamp near
Kennedy Space Center's Astronaut Hall of Fame. Here's the message:
Message:
Hi there, Just wanted to let you know that we found your box on
December 20, 2007. And... WHILE I WAS STAMPING, my husband
says, "Joanne, look up!" and there, up in the sky, was a rocket! How
cool is that! Thanks for the memory!

Reminds me of the time I found a hitchhiker called "Julie's 40"
celebrating her 40th's birthday. I found it on my 40th birthday!

I'm sure some of you have similar stories...please share

Okay, here is one that happened this last summer. We were on our
summer 'escape the heat' trip. We had planted a few, found a bunch, but
hadn't planted very many around the area south of Hood River. We were
on our way back from some really cool letterboxes around a small lake
when we spotted a sign for a waterfall.

I had precarved a pretty neat stamp so we pulled off the main road and
headed up to the waterfall.
The road ended at an old CCC made bridge. The road was blocked by some
large boulders. We parked and checked out the falls that were just
above the bridge with the creek formed by the falls flowing under the
bridge.

The falls were pretty spectacular and the old bridge was pretty neat
and I started looking around for a planting site. I found a large prone
log laying next to the road. That log would be easily identifiable so I
started poking around the log. At the down hill side of the very end of
the log I slipped and went down on my butt. I layed there for a second
or two just surveying any damage. I checked my hand and there was some
brown goo. Closer inspection revealed that it wasn't brown goo.

You guessed it..it wasn't goo. it was poop. Not just any poop either,
but this was of the human kind. Pretty stinky too.
Getting up rather quickly now, I went over to our truck and asked Gwen
if she would get me the paper towels and some water. She helped me wash
the stuff off of my hands.
She then asked if I had an extra pair of pants, and looking down saw a
small spot on the front. I told her I could wash that off, and her
reply was "do you have an extra pair of pants?".

I dug out the extra shorts I had and changed pants finally seeing the
back side of the pants I was wearing..yep it was covered!

Putting them into a trashbag, they went into the back of the truck.

Moral...Don't poke around on the downhill side of prone logs.


The letterbox didn't get planted. It would have required a different
image for the stamp.

Gwen was really cool she didn't even crack a smile.

Don
