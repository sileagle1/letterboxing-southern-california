Griffith Park -

It is indeed huge and full of far too many places to plant boxes for any
sane individual... or group for that matter. I suggested it as a "maybe next
year" project when it was brought up with our group. On the weekends, it is
often very crowded in the picnic areas and hiking trails, particularly the
ones that go where vehicles are banned. During the week, it is practically
dead except near the zoo and playground near the merry-go-round, where
school groups can be found. I would strongly advise against going there at
night alone - or even with others - it's a different and dangerous place,
despite the best efforts of the park rangers to clean it up.

My main concern about Griffith would be it's size. We could restrict things
to just one area to ensure we do run into one another, but that loses some
of the excitement of the place. If the boxes are spread out so that a good
portion of the park's areas can be visited, we may not see one another - as
Buzzard already pointed out. Have we considered a moving boxing gathering?
We pre-arrange that at certain times, we'll meet in key areas to swap stamps
and get clues to boxes planted nearby. Just a thought on how to make that
park work.

Brookside Park -

I too have never explored it on foot, though I've driven through areas of it
and found it fairly quiet except on event days at the Rose Bowl. If others
are interested, I don't mind making a drive out there myself to look for
possible boxing locations.

In either case, I'm excited about the idea of a local gathering, pretty much
regardless of the park selected, so long as it's the greater LA area :) I've
yet to talk any of the kids into going, but perhaps once I get someone (Nik
is the most involved) hyped about planting special boxes, the interest will
overpower the shyness.

Annette
Gnosis 4-H Club

Annette,

Right you are--we wouldn't want to hang around Griffith park after
dark, but that goes for a lot of parks. You're right about the size
of Griffith Park being both a strength and a weakness: many
interesting hiding places, but we may not see each other all day.
Brookside Park is of a much more manageable size, but may not have
enough diversity--a lot of the park is taken up by a golf course--
and, thanks to Don & Gwen, we already have stamp golf going on in
Long Beach. I live in Pasadena, so Brookside is right in my back
yard, and easy for me to check out and report. An advantage that
both parks have is they do not charge for parking.
--Buzzard
