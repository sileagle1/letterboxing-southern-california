I've been busy lately, and I just want to officially state that I
prefer Speedy Stamp over Master Carve. Although the thickness
eliminates the need for a backing, it still has a little too much
crumble factor when doing the details. The only thing that holds up
better than the pink stuff is the orange stuff, but it's TOO hard and
slick, so doesn't hold ink very well for stamping.

Just had to express my two cents. It was frustrating that my "Venus"
turned out a lot better than I expected I could do, but I still lost a
nick in her shoulder.

Kelsung

The only thing that holds up
> better than the pink stuff is the orange stuff, but it's TOO hard and
> slick, so doesn't hold ink very well for stamping.

Interesting how different carvers like different material. My first
choice is white PZKut grade A, second choice is PZKut white B and
thirdly PZKut orange. I really dislike Master Carve and if I can
remember to bring the piece with me on the meet and greet then someone
can have it. Can that be a bribe for Sunday?????

Getting Grade A white can be a problem as Stampeaz doesn't have it all
the time, and when they do have it, it doesn't stay around long.

With any of the PZKuts you have to keep your nibs sharpened before and
during carving.

The surface can be a problem with either one but using acetone scrubb
just prior to making a transfer of the image onto the carving medium
does more than help the transfer. It sets the surface up for a better
inking.
Another technique for fixing the surface is to use 600 wet or dry
sandpaper lightly on the stamps surface.

I like to use the acetone BEFORE carving as if it is used after carving
it can screw up a stamp as the acetone actually eats into the rubber
material.

Don

From: gwendontoo (foxsecurity@earthlink.net) | Date: 2008-12-08 19:03:22 UTC
The sanding won't help transfer. It will help to keep ink from balling
up. The acetone scrub method will help transfers. I use it even when I
do a pencil transfer.

If ink is either balling up or caking then you might want to try
another type of ink pad.

From: gwendontoo (foxsecurity@earthlink.net) | Date: 2008-12-09 16:49:57 UTC
Order and use them all!

Webfoot's (Stampeaz owner)carving kit is a super way to get to know
which medium you like best. That includes tools as well, Speedball,
Exacto, Staedler.

You should get some of the "Pink Stuff" as well.

The only carving medium that I wouldn't reccomend you getting is
Plumber's gasket. I've seen some folks try that stuf and it is
horrible.

Different mediums should be tried. For those soft ones, placing it in
the frig can change it a bit also.

Next time you want a cheese sandwich try carving some Kraft American
cheese squares.

Don
