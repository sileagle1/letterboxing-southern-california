From: Gwen & Don Jackson (foxsecurity@earthlink.net) | Date: 2003-10-24 16:52:03 UTC-07:00
Obviously work gets in the way of every thing that is fun. Well about crispy
boxes. With the McNally fire that burned about 300,000 square acres and
nearly took out the 100 giants (actually there is many more than 100), we
had at least one box right in the middle of the conflagration. "No Rubber
Duckies" was hidden under a good sized rock and survived. The box wasn't
even melted. Forest fires are very strange. Four years ago we had a very
close call here in Kernville. Just a few days prior to July 4, some idiotic
kid shot off some fire crackers about 400 yds ( that's yards not ells )
below our house. We ran for our lives, actually drove. The fire literally
engulfed the entire area around the house and Gwen & I watched it and Kissed
the house off. With the proper weed whacking the fire went around. We feel
pretty blessed!!!!!! That's my fire story. Don

From: trentlaudes (trentlaudes@excite.com) | Date: 2003-10-26 00:15:47 UTC
Gwen & Don,
That's an amazing story about your own house being nearly ingulfed by
a fire--that is so scary. Our house was not as threatened as yours,
but a great early 90's fire in our local San Gabriel Mountains did
destroy homes five blocks away from us, and did devastate Eaton
Canyon, where some of my letterboxes are now hidden (not hidden at
the time of the fire). Wildfires are part of the price we pay for
living in these beautiful areas. There are no wildfires in downtown
Los Angeles, which is far from the rugged, beautiful mountains.
--Buzzard
