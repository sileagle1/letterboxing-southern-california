From: kelsung42 (kelsung42@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-12-22 07:08:43 UTC
I was adding up my new totals, and it got me to thinking about how
differently I imagine various boxers track their numbers. Obviously,
it's an informal thing, and some don't bother to keep track at all,
but I have a bit of an obsessive personality (in case you hadn't
noticed by now) so I was curious what other people's thoughts were.

P 55
This includes all my traditionals, hitchhikers, and now a personal
traveler.
F 411
This includes traditionals and personal travelers. My logic is that
in most cases I had to figure out some kind of clue to get the
traveler, I just had to find the person, not a box location.
X 74
This is the one number I'm not confident is accurate. For example: I
have "Don and Gwen's" big stamp, but I also have both Don's fat duck
hunter and Gwen's penguin, as well as Maggie the Dog. So is this one
exchange, two (for two people) or four (four total stamps)? Also, I
just got new stamps from Chokato, LilRainDrop and Gerwaiian Oma (but
not Tdyans, darn it!) but I have their old ones. I wouldn't count
that as a new exchange, but I suppose some might (it is, after all, a
new stamp image I've collected).
HH 25
This includes all hitchhikers I've found. I don't include these in my
P count, because I stumbled on them as a bonus, rather than seeking
them out via clues.
EV 16
This includes all event stamps, including the several traveling ones I
got at the Huntington Beach gathering, and cooties as well, since I'm
only ever going to get one of those at an event, and it seemed silly
to keep a separate count for such a small part of the overall hobby.

Does anyone have any counter-logic as to how they would total theirs
differently?

The last gathering put me over the 200 mark, and thanks to PBandJ's
travelers (I got 17 out of 23!) this gathering put me over the 400
mark. Still a far cry from the 10,000+ Wanda and Pete have. I'm
jealous of retirees.

Kelsung

From: gwendontoo (foxsecurity@earthlink.net) | Date: 2008-12-22 16:47:53 UTC
Well Grasshopper the first thing you have to do is reduce your
personality type from AAA down to a single A. Once you do that then
you will give up your obsessiveness about counting anything other
than how much $ you have to spend. Retirement is not all a bed of
roses. Just think how we feel everytime I look at where the S&P is
these days.
Now since you were mentioning Pete and Wanda, their last count was
something over 13000, so why bother?

Now you have a idea why we don't bother to count anymore. We just
enjoy the process and that is one of the real beauties of
Letterboxing. Mark Pepe came up with the best analogy that
letterboxing is like an onion, some folks peel back several layers,
and others just find that layer that they are comfortable with. The
real joy for Gwen and Don is the folks.

It was great seeing all of the new/old faces, and Kudos to Piper Paws
for a great Holiday get-to-gether.

Merry Christmas to all!!!!!

Don
