From: kelsung42 (kelsung42@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-12-29 09:39:44 UTC
Of my few jaunts to that area, one of my favorites was the South
Pasadena Series. Some of them are missing, but #4 would be cool for
kids (if they can be discreet) and even if #5 is missing, the location
would be fun.

http://letterboxing.org/BoxView.php?boxnum=7104

Sadly, the Hahamongna Series appear to be mostly missing, because
those would be great for kids.

http://letterboxing.org/BoxView.php?boxnum=22182

Further beyond those in that same park you would also find #11 in The
Halloween Series, but I would NOT recommend that one for kids.

http://letterboxing.org/BoxView.php?boxnum=6223

There are some longer hikes in that area that I just haven't gotten to
yet, like Millard Falls or Coooper Canyon, but I will eventually.

Kelsung

From: Curtis & Paula H. (8hands4jesus@dslextreme.com) | Date: 2008-12-29 18:34:18 UTC
Hi Bloomin Gramma Jo --

We haven't explored as far as Pasadena yet, but if you do an Arcadia
search, you'll find a couple of letterboxes in the L.A. County
Arboraetum. I plan on introducing a friend and her tribe of 4 kids to
letterboxing at the Arboraetum tomorrow, so I'll let you know how it
goes. We tried to find one of the boxes there, a year ago, without
success, and I was too "green" to have planned ahead and brought a
second set of clues along. Even without the success of a find, we had
a wonderful day (I have 2 elementary kids, myself) there. We'll be
trying that one box again, I think -- since others have found it after
me. (Will have to doublecheck on that.)

So glad you'll get to head out this way and visit your kids!

Blessings,

hansenclan
(Covina, CA)
