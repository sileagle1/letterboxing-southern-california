These days, we tend to think of out cities as pretty fireproof and consider ourselves immune from events such as the Great Chicago Fire and the fire after the San Francisco earthquake. I recall an event about 10-15 years ago that proves that wrong. It was during a time of high Santa Ana winds that an apartment fire broke out in Anaheim and consumed every building in a six-block area. It was quite staggering to the mind to see it happening right in front of you when we consider that kind of thing to be something of the past.

The strange thing is that we are getting ash falling from the sky here in the Long Beach area. My wife (who has a very sensitive sniffer) has been smelling smoke for the past two days.
