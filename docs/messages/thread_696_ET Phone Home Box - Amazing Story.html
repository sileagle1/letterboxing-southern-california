ET Phone Home Box - Amazing Story

From: geishagirl29127 (geishagirl29127@hotmail.com) | Date: 2009-01-13 02:35:56 UTC
Hi All,

Many of you know of my E.T. Phone Home Box. It is in an area that
had recently been hit by some of the LA fires. I had gotten reports
about how the box may have been gone...until today. Check out this
amazing email that I received from KC of Team Serra's Silver... you
can also see pics on KC's blog:
http://momilosophies.blogspot.com/2009/01/how-to-avoid-building-
mission-by-taking.html. Enjoy, and a million thank you's KC!

""I am walking along this small concrete ditch reading the clue as I
go -- 'lala la la la" and as I turn a corner, my mouth drops open.
It's apocalypto. EVERYTHING is burned. Ground black, bushes and trees
just black stalks. It's so weird -- and to top it off it's dry and
windy with the Santa Ana's blowing. And Cold. Thank God I think, so
cold it's a small chance of fire right now. Just a few feet back and
it's a lovely green grass park, turn a corner and it's McCarthy's The
Road. I can't recall what fire it was but I know it was this season
since I remember the freeway closure and I know that our hills
regenerate fast -- there is no green. So I am supposed to follow this
ditch to it's end, where it ends in shrubbery. I'll be looking at a
pink house. All I can see is blackness -- burned out black and yuck.
OH and the pink house? GONE. I see it's fence/deck still hanging over
the edge of it precipice, but it's gone. Shrubbery? A few black
stalks sticking out of the ground. I see that the edge of the
concrete has broken in the heat and tumbled down the hill a ways. I
search under the edges of the concrete and see not even a melted
trace of letterbox. I keep looking. I shimmy down the hillside a
little bit to see if the broken concrete piece will budge -- no
doing. I stand up in the drainage ditch after checking both sides
again and think CRAP, this thing is just gone. I kept checking
though. Finally I think to pull the clue back out of my pocket and re-
read it. Sure enough it says to look for green graffiti and then look
under the edge where you find it. Hell girl, the graffiti was melted
off the concrete! But I could see trace. So one part of green has
sand up to the edge. I trace back to the end of the ditch and can
barely make out more green. Finally I find a hole (no concrete rock
hiding it anymore as per clue) and there lies the melted remains of a
letterbox. I pull it out -- it's a mass of plastic melt. BUT --
here's the crazy thing: right in the center of the melt is a
perfectly intact, unmelted stamp!!!!! Can you believe it? There is
one tiny piece of unburned paper towel, but other than that
everything is black and burned or melted. Except the stamp!! It's all
bright and colorful. LOL I am so delighted! I brought it home in a
bag. Going to take pictures before I disassemble it and stamp with
it." So, I have the stamp and the melted box (sorry, hardly a trace
of the logbook in it) and if you don't mind I would like to stamp my
logbook with the amazing fire-proof stamp, before mailing it back. I
will take photos, before and after and will mail all to you (or just
the stamp if you would like) so you can replace it. It was a great
place to hide it. Who knew the fire would come so close?"
