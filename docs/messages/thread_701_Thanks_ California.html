Thanks, California

From: Mary Jo (mjrekedal@gmail.com) | Date: 2009-01-26 22:38:45 UTC
Just returned home from 10 beautiful days in lovely southern
California. Wanted to say thanks to all you wonderful Cal boxers. I
had hoped to spend several days on the trails while grandkids were in
school, but with cheerleading, drama practice and dance lessons to
attend, l'boxing took a back seat.
Did manage one day out climbing Echo Mountain. Wow! Well worth the
climb. It was just Gramma and Mom with 2 pre-teens and a 5-yr-old,
and the dog, as Dad was away on business. Which meant we had to
figure a way to coax the 5yo up the hill, not having daddy's
shoulders to ride on. Back packs filled with picnic and lots of
water, jogging stroller borrowed from friend (up the mountain? are
you crazy?), off we went.
Counted steps to Echo Mountain Box #1, and stamped in while trail was
miraculously empty. Mom pushed that stroller over rock and stump, 5yo
riding on the smooth sections, dog taking a break or two when the
stroller was empty.
Made it to the top, and the view was well worth all the effort.
Wonderful! It was one of those beautiful clear blue days with gentle
breeze that can only be found in southern Calif.
Duly located Box #2 and #3, altho #3 missing stamp and log book. Also
found a little rabbit hiding in a hole with a HH snuggled with him.
Read all the info posts, maraveled at the ruins, and shouted our
echos to the mountain in the echo phone. Great fun.
Trekking back down the mountain found a place to plant another box to
add to the interest. Here is link if anyone is interested.
http://www.atlasquest.com/showinfo.html?gBoxId=103883.
Thanks to Koritzke family and VIENNA for your plants, and to all you
on this list who gave suggestions on where to find boxes while on my
visit.
Bloomin Gramma Jo in Texas
