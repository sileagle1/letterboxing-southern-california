How important is it to have a really well carved stamp? My first two boxes (one of which is about ready to be placed) have my first official stamp carvings. Re: stamp quality

From: Curtis & Paula H. (8hands4jesus@dslextreme.com) | Date: 2009-06-13 22:29:00 UTC
re: My question is, are you offended when you get a box with a crude stamp...?

Absolutely not! While I'm not thrilled with store-bought stamps, I've only been letterboxing about 18 months, and used a store-bought stamp for my first 11 months. My current signature stamp is my first carving and is really nothing more than an eraser with "Hansenclan" carved into it. I finally began to learn carving for participation in Postal Letterboxes, and have carved 11 stamps now, both for postals and also for five boxes that I'm planning on planting.

All of us fall somewhere within quite a range of talent and experience, and I think all of us appreciate any effort made. That includes someone planting with a storebought stamp: the effort to create clues and a fun letterboxing experience has still been made.

Your efforts, crude or refined, are always welcomed. My "postal" friends were always very gracious and encouraging about my stamps, and never left me feeling I should have done a better job. Even after I saw some of THEIR magnificent carvings!

I'm glad you're making the effort, and wish we lived nearby so we could see your carvings and search for your box(es) ourselves.

Blessings,

hansenclan
(Southern California)
