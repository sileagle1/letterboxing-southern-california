To relocate or not?

From: dafamilyishere (robinsends@yahoo.com) | Date: 2009-07-07 03:26:19 UTC
I was checking on my boxes today, and doing some planning for expansion when I came to the only box that has yet to be found, Rodeo Time. The box had obviously been found because the location was slightly different from where I had placed it. Also, all the items inside the bag were in a different order from where I had originally placed them. I was very grateful that the finder returned the box, though a bit concerned that an apparent muggle had already discovered the box. I was contemplating a new hiding spot and looking for other places to hide boxes because I want to turn this box into a series, and I came across a geocache. It was not more than 15 feet from my box. I think that perhaps a geocacher might have been looking for the other box and run across mine.
Now, here is the question. Do you think I should relocate this box? If it was muggled, that would mean that it was such an obvious spot that it was found by muggles within two weeks of hiding it. If, as I suspect, it was a geocacher trying to find the cache I found, then I might not have to worry about it. This is my favorite stamp so far (except for my personal traveler), but I really love the location where it is. What do you think? Relocate, or no?


Re: To relocate or not?

From: Lee & Nancy (luckielee@aol.com) | Date: 2009-07-07 04:36:46 UTC
I would definately relocate. You can keep it in the same general area, but I would move it a good 100 feet or more from the geocache, or it will be plagued by unintended finders. I too have had boxes messed with do to accidently placing too close to a geocache.

Lee & Nancy
