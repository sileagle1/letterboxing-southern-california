cheapo depot brother printer and transfer business

From: aaaltomare (aaaltomare@gmail.com) | Date: 2009-07-23 15:58:20 UTC
I have decided to take the plunge and buy a cheapy deepy laser printer. I found, on staples.com a Brother that comes out to be just $50.

http://www.staples.com/office/supplies/p4_Brother-Refurbished-HL-2140-Laser-Printer_283682_Business_Supplies_1_10051_SC3:CG44:DP1606:CL142045

from the reviews I've read, it seems to be perfect for general fast printing of transferable images and maybe a few clue sheets, though I am trying to figure out an efficient way to go paperless while boxing (for the clues, that is).

I've also found a new way to do the transfer, well new to me anyways. Before, I would use blender markers or xylol. People also recommended acetone. All of these I have found pretty much unacceptable. I've found that laying the image to be transferred down and then brushing a very light coat of laquer thinner on the back transfers the image almost immediately without using a burnisher of some kind. Before playing with thinner, I had given up doing xerox transfers, exclusively drawing and doing a pencil transfer, but now I may be back on the wagon, so to speak.
