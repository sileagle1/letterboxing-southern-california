From: Kelsung (kelsung42@yahoo.com) | Date: 2010-12-02 06:07:15 UTC
> Kelsung -- does that mean that SOMEBODY has actually found all the pieces, decyphered them, and found the final box? (While I remain stuck at 13 clues collected.) WOW.

Umm... You are referring to Alphabet Soup, and yes, Bumblebeebunny found it on 3/8/09, a mere 10 days after I posted the clue. Of course, that's because they had been gathering pieces for two months beforehand as I was sneaking them out, not yet knowing what they were for.

Sand And Snow, the first finders on my Sound Guy box, which originally required you to actually come meet me at the bar I was working in at the time, were so intrigued by the string of letters inside, they focused on little else for a while and became the second finders of Alphabet Soup on 4/15/09.

It has sat dormant ever since, but I know it won't be forever. Buffalo Tony, Alarch and a few others, including you, have actively sought out quite a few pieces.

But back to what THIS thread is talking about: Since BBB found Soup so "easily" (they actually worked pretty hard to pull that off) I needed vengeance! So, I created the Puzzle Master series. I hoped many would be intrigued by the puzzles and seek them out, but I honestly had only one goal in mind: to stump BBB.

Within six weeks, they had found eight of the nine required boxes, with a failed attempt on the ninth. They remain the ONLY finders on three of them. (Actually, that isn't true: all three have been stumbled upon accidentally by one noxer each, who all actually logged in and put the box back where they found it.)

However, with the excuse of pregnancy (I insist upon calling it an "excuse") and "cheating" because the grandparents went and found that ninth box for them, they still took sixteen months to figure out the bonus clues and actually find the "Puzzle Master" box.

Quite an accomplishment indeed. I think both of us are satisfied: I for coming up with such a diabolical puzzle, and they for cracking it.

Of course, MrOspital will be on their heels if the light bulb ever goes off with that Bridge clue that is driving him insane. And I Dig Toasters has been working from afar on The Heavens and $ in hopes she can find them when she visits.

Kelsung

I realize this post has been even more self-indulgent than usual, but for anyone interested, a while back I put together a sort of "cheat-sheet" on my website:

http://www.kelsung.com/letterboxing/PuzzleMaster.html
