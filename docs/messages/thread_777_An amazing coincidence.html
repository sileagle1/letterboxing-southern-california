An amazing coincidence

From: Old Roar (old_roar@yahoo.com) | Date: 2009-10-08 06:22:28 UTC
Wow, this amazing thing happened today!

On Sept. 15, I planted my second letterbox, at Shadow Ranch Park in West Hills. Since then I visited it twice to make minor modifications, but nobody else stamped in until Oct. 2, after which I received notification on Atlas Quest. So today, at around 3 p.m., I took time off from work to visit the box a third time to see the stamp.

As I walked from my car toward the box, I saw a woman and two teenage boys get up from the area where I'd planted the box and walk in my direction. The woman was carrying a black book. I thought, "these people must be letterboxers!"

So I asked: "Excuse me, were you just visiting my letterbox?"

"Yes, we were," the lady replied. "Are you Old Roar?"

It turns out this was the third letterbox they tried to find today -- and their first successful find ever! They asked me to sign their book to record the moment.

What are the odds of this sort of thing happening? Has anything like this happened to anyone else here?

Wow! Running into another letterboxer on the trail is rare enough, at least in so cal, but for them to bump into the PLANTER of the box on their very FIRST FIND will be an awesome thing for them to remember!

This just reminds me of my early days of planting, when I would check LbNA every day to see if anyone had found one, and go out and visit my boxes ever couple weeks. I've definitely settled down on that.
