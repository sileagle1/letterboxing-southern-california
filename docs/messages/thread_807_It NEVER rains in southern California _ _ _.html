It NEVER rains in southern California . . .

From: dezertratty (fldnpdls@yahoo.com) | Date: 2010-01-20 16:47:33 UTC

So I took my daughter down to CSULB yesterday, and since she was going
to be several hours (new job) - I thought I would kill time by doing a
little LBing in between the sprinkles - HA!

After checking out the wrong location on one LB in Seal Beach - I
decided to try out another, one closer to the water . . . about that
time God gave Noah the heads up on the ARK design and the deluge began.

I was able to travel from Seal Beach Blvd. to the 405 and onto the
22/7th street exit by sheer nerve, white knuckles, and lots of squinting
and prayers.

I thought if I could just make it back to CSULB, I could take refuge in
the campus parking lot - down by the kids' dorm, and wait out the
storm -boy was I wrong!

After turning onto W. Campus Drive I immediately realized I had made a
mistake . . . they should have named it W. River Drain.

The workers by the poof ball waterfall (my son's name for the fountains
on campus that look like - well - poof balls) were working frantically
trying to keep the deluge water from flowing over the curb and into the
Admin. Bldg. while directing traffic around their equipment and,
subsequently, had me turn and head back up stream.

Which went pretty well until I hit 7th Street again.

In the few minutes I was gone, navigating the river on campus, the
waters had risen and a lake was now in front of the Veteran's Hospital!

I was so glad to see folks keeping their heads and moving slowly! Whew.

I was able to motor slowly across and touch dry asphalt in the turn
lane - and, since the turn lane was dry, I turned.

I made my way into the Ralph's Center parking lot where I sought out
high ground and waited.

I was just considering climbing into the back and knocking out for a
little while - when what to my wondering eyes should appear?? Blue Sky!

Hmmmmmmm, I pondered, should I at least try and find one of Kelsung's
while waiting??

So off down Bellflower I ventured . . . . . Neither rain, nor flood, nor
tornado (well, maybe the tornado) shall keep this LBer from her
appointed find.

I've lost it, I know :-P

The most amazing thing was, that by the time I went back to pick my
daughter up, all the water was gone! Even the lake in front of the Vets
Hospital had vanished???

Amazing things - drains . . .hmmmm. What I want to know is who
pulled the plug?



Dezert Ratty

I thought if I could just make it back to CSULB, I could take refuge in
the campus parking lot - down by the kids' dorm, and wait out the
storm -boy was I wrong!

After turning onto W. Campus Drive I immediately realized I had made a
mistake . . . they should have named it W. River Drain.

The workers by the poof ball waterfall (my son's name for the fountains
on campus that look like - well - poof balls) were working frantically
trying to keep the deluge water from flowing over the curb and into the
Admin. Bldg. while directing traffic around their equipment and,
subsequently, had me turn and head back up stream.

Which went pretty well until I hit 7th Street again.

In the few minutes I was gone, navigating the river on campus, the
waters had risen and a lake was now in front of the Veteran's Hospital!

I was so glad to see folks keeping their heads and moving slowly! Whew.

I was able to motor slowly across and touch dry asphalt in the turn
lane - and, since the turn lane was dry, I turned.

I made my way into the Ralph's Center parking lot where I sought out
high ground and waited.

I was just considering climbing into the back and knocking out for a
little while - when what to my wondering eyes should appear?? Blue Sky!

Hmmmmmmm, I pondered, should I at least try and find one of Kelsung's
while waiting??

So off down Bellflower I ventured . . . . . Neither rain, nor flood, nor
tornado (well, maybe the tornado) shall keep this LBer from her
appointed find.

I've lost it, I know :-P

The most amazing thing was, that by the time I went back to pick my
daughter up, all the water was gone! Even the lake in front of the Vets
Hospital had vanished???

Amazing things - drains . . .hmmmm. What I want to know is – who
pulled the plug?

Dezert Ratty
