Famous bikers and hikers

From: dezertratty (fldnpdls@yahoo.com) | Date: 2010-05-23 04:53:33 UTC
Well the good ol' high desert has been buzzing with athlete celebs - the latest victims of the Station Fire.

Friday the "Tour of California" left from Palmdale, went down Historic Route 6 and up into the Mountains.
Seems, due to the Station Fire, and the subsequent damage to Hwy. 2, they could not leave from the beautiful downtown area of Pasadena and had to come up here, to my home turf area, and leave from Palmdale (to which I even say ewww) - thank goodness for the abundant rainfall that "greened" the area up!

Coming down out of the Mountains, due to damage by the Station Fire, are athletes of a different variety - the PCT Hikers. I passed several on Friday, in my race to CSULB (MOM, my RA let me know I have to be out by 5, you have to come now!!), and wondered if Green Tortuga was among them??

I had to stay over in Long Beach due to a bike ride of a different sort today - the Boy Scouts had a 25/50/100 miler - and I got "stationed" at Legg Lake handing out refreshments - which, by the way, is a pretty nifty park and very "Box Worthy" (hint hint) AND, when I got home, I saw an article in the paper that made me chuckle - evidently the residents of the Juniper Hills area have been a little nervous about all these people with scruffy appearances and backpacks coming down out of the mountains . . . they have been assured by the Juniper Hills Town Council President that "the hikers are generally quite conservation-minded folks, and are simply passing through" - (this from the man that stopped his recumbent bike in front of a Scout today causing him to endo over his handelbars and do a major face plant just prior to getting to Legg Lake today!)

Anyway - I emailed Ryan and asked if he was one of those folks and he says he just got to Wrightwood, so it wasn't him.
So maybe I'll see an LB Celeb walking down the road if I keep my eyes out, who knows! I wonder if he'd do an exchange :->

Dezert Ratty

PS: the kid that did the face plant was all smiles and dripping blood when he pulled in - it's just one more great story to him :-P

The 2 is closed between La Canada and Red Box. The news today reports that the various "Tujunga Canyon" roads are closed. There seems to be conflicting info coming from different sources 'cause the Mt Wilson Observatory website refers you to an alternate route using Tujunga Cyn. If there is some back way open to the observatory, you'd be going very near Vienna's Strawberry Peak box.

Switzer Falls is definitely closed. I found Hamish McB's box 5 years ago (a store-bought stamp, but a rather nice one). FYI, in case they ever re-open, my notes mention my dog falling into the water crossing the stream, and if you arrive after the parking lot fills up, it's a long uphill walk back to your car in the secondary parking.
I'm really hoping some of these boxes were able to survive, so keep us posted, and good luck!
