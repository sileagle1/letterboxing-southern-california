From: Kelsung (kelsung42@yahoo.com) | Date: 2010-06-11 15:59:24 UTC
Letterboxes are generally frowned upon on private property without permission of the owners. I think amusement parks would certainly fall into that category. However, I seem to be in a minority on this opinion.

There have been many boxes that have come and gone at Disneyland over the years, and there is currently a lot of them there, mostly by Vienna, though I don't know how many are still viable. They have many finds, so boxers certainly show an interest in them.

Other parks, however, don't seem to hold the same fascination as Disney. There are only a couple at Universal Studios, one that I think is missing at Knott's, and none I know of at Wild Rivers or Magic Mountain.

Kelsung
