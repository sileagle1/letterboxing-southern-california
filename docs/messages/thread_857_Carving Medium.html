Carving Medium

From: Alarch (alarch2@yahoo.com) | Date: 2010-08-12 20:46:59 UTC
FYI, Swain's has a new shipment of the pink stuff, if anyone needs some or just wants to make a field trip to a great independent art supply store. If you bring kids be forewarned they have an irresistible children's dept! Puppets, Eboo, kits, books... ; )

They also had something I haven't seen before called "Moo Carve". It's grey and 1/2 " thick (midway between Master Carve and the pink stuff.) I bought a 4x4 block ( $3.99) to try out and discovered it's actually made from 2 2x4 blocks with a fine weld running up the center, likely not good for large images. I did some research, word is that it carves easily but can be crumbly. I haven't carved it yet.

Has anyone tried this or heard of it before?

p.s. If you're going to Swain's call first as they have a huge sale around Sept. each year,
great deals but expect LONG lines on weekends during that sale!

From: Adam (clarinet_226@yahoo.com) | Date: 2010-08-22 02:10:39 UTC
I've carved Moo Carve. I got I 2x4 block of it, and it is kinda like a cross between Speedy Carve and Speedy Cut. It does crumble like Speedy Cut, but not as much, and its not so powdery, and its a lot better than speedy cut. In fact I used it to carve the stamp for my FRANKLIN LAKE" box. I even made a HH called "MOO CARVE HH"

Clarinet 226
