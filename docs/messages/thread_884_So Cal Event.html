So Cal Event

From: Kelsung (kelsung42@yahoo.com) | Date: 2011-02-12 02:44:02 UTC
Okay, fellow So Cal boxers, I can contain myself no longer: who wants to play "Clue"? I've been slowly planning an idea for an event/game for a while, and it's time to see who would be interested in playing.

The basic idea is to have six teams (Plum, Mustard, etc.) going after 21 boxes, each containing a stamp of a card, narrowing down which three are not going to be found. The first team to figure out the final solution will get clues to the final box. These will all be temporary plants for a one-day event.

Actually, there's more to it than that, but I want to see who's up for it before I bother getting into details. I'm thinking either April 2nd or 9th at a park in Orange County.

Kelsung

It'd be great to have you. Based on two posts on AQ, I'm leaning towards the 9th. I will post an event listing there, but I want to wait a little while, because there will be a limited number of people allowed, obviously.

It will be six teams (Scarlett, Mustard, White, Green, Peacock, Plum) and ideally I'm thinking 3 or 4 people each. Minimum of two, because the servants will be whispering bonus clues to you and you may want to split up as the game progresses. Two couples (or two pairs of platonic friends) would probably be ideal.

So start thinking now which team you'd like to be and with whom, because once I post the event listing, it will be first-come first-served. I don't want to hurt any feelings, but that's just the only way it will work. And I think it would be cool if everyone wore matching color T-shirts!

First, because I want this to move in a timely fashion. It will be a competition for which team can figure out the final solution first.

Second, I'll need to provide a specific logbook to players, allowing one stamp per page. Because, you know, you may have to show a card to another team. I only want to make two per team (in case you split up).

Third, since the trails will be close to one another (even overlapping) you want small groups, to minimize one team seeing where another may be looking for a box. Don't want any cheating.

As you can see, OCD nut-job that I am, I've already put a lot of thought into this. I started hatching the idea in early December. I've already got most of the stamps carved and most of the clues written.

Kelsung

Wow! I knew this game idea would spark some interest, but I had no idea it would bring so many out of the woodwork! I'd be honored to have you and your husband, Tara. I have fond memories of finding Travis' Box and Barbara's Box in my early days of boxing.
