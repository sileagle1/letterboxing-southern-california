Your memory of the hike is accurate: easy until that last hill. The boxes there are nice and the waterfall is totally worth it. There is also another somewhat forgotten box there. But it's a hike in the other direction and probably best left for a second trip:

http://www.atlasquest.com/showinfo.html?gBoxId=46334

> 2. Eaton Canyon (above Altadena) -- Have never been there, and never heard about it until it showed up on the Letterboxing Radar. Curious, but don't know how strenuous/long it is, compared to #1, above.

Well, depending which boxes you are going for, this starts very easy. If you're going to the Threedigs box adopted by California Bear, that will be a short, easy hike. If you add to that the Walnut Canyon box from Buzzard's "Other Canyons" series, which is nearby, that hike gets a little hairy toward the end. But both are worth it just for the age of the boxes alone.

But if you want to continue all the way to the falls and the Hummingbird box, that's a much longer and more difficult hike. Not really that difficult of a hike, but relative to the first half. And this box is all uphill from Eaton with very little shade:

http://letterboxing.org/BoxView.php?boxnum=56663

So, in summary, if it's a one-time shot to keep the noxers interested, I'd go with Chantry Flats. But keep Eaton Canyon on your to-do list. Buffalo Tony actually planted a box along the way when we went to get the Hummingbird last year, but he never posted the clues, so I may need to make that one a word-of-mouth box.

Eaton canyon is beautiful and there is a place to get you closer to the falls than parking near the nature center. This cuts out most of the non-shaded part of the hike. But don't go right after heavy rains as the places where you cross the water won't be so easy.

(Kelsung - please pass on BTony's clues, as I am going back soon!)

2260 Pinecrest Dr. Altadena, CA
To get to the Pinecrest gate, go north on Altadena Drive one mile past the entrance to Eaton Canyon. Turn right on Crescent, then right again on Pinecrest. Observe parking signs carefully.

Hiking directions :
Pass through the gate and down the road about 0.2 mile. Cross the bridge and veer right on path (but you will end up going left at ground level). You can use the steep path immediately on the right to descend directly from the end of bridge to the canyon-bottom trail, or you can continue on the road briefly to find a couple other routes that are more gentle. Once at the canyon-bottom trail, proceed up canyon under the bridge. As you continue up canyon, the route crosses the creek many times, tied together by short stretches of rustic trail.
