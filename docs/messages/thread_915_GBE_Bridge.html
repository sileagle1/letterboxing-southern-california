Yes, we hugged the wall on the way up, and the bush was at the end. The next line says to get behind it, and be careful if you go ON the overpass, which is just what I did. From: Jon Bain (jsbain@yahoo.com) | Date: 2011-06-21 09:37:35 UTC-07:00
I gathered from the clue that it was supposed to be right over the yellow lines on the road.. oh well -- next time someone will have to bring a pickaxe :)
 
By the way, I sent a question to the ranger district after our little... shall we say discussion ... with the ranger.
 
Here was the response (in case anyone else is planning to take this trip while Buckhorn campground is still closed)
 
---- Forwarded Message -----
From: "Wagner, John"
To: "jsbain@yahoo.com"
Sent: Monday, June 20, 2011 12:55 PM
Subject: RE: WWW Mail: Is Burkhart trail open or closed?

Hi Jon,

I will forward your concerns about the rangers  needing to be informed:

Here is info I received about the Burkhart Trail:

The Burkhardt Trail is open, but we are trying to dissuade people from accessing it from Buckhorn Campground as it is still an active construction site.  However, according to Mike McIntyre, the Buckhorn Campground is not administratively closed, so hikers can technically access the Burkhardt Trail through the campground.  To prevent any confusion and to curtail any safety issues, I have been suggesting that people access the Burkhardt Trail/Cooper Canyon Falls via Cloudburst Summit.

The Burkhardt Trail is considered to be within the Santa Clara/Mojave Rivers Ranger District, but we administer the trail since it is logistically easier for us to access. (same as Buckhorn Campground)

John D. Wagner, M.A.
Assistant Public Affairs Officer
Angeles National Forest
U.S. Forest Service
(626) 574-5206
