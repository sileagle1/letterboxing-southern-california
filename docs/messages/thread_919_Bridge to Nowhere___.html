Bridge to Nowhere...

From: WaterLossGuy (8hands4jesus@dslextreme.com) | Date: 2011-07-07 22:39:03 UTC
On Saturday, either my hubby (if his back is well enough) or I will accompany our son, Little Puppy,on a hike with his Boy Scout Troop. The hike is called "Bridge to Nowhere" and begins at East Fork of Azusa Canyon. I thought there might be a letterbox there. Just curious, even though with the Troop along, I probably won't be free to look for a letterbox.

After recovering from the shock I experienced when I misread the link provided to us for the hike (I thought it was 10.1 miles one way, and that's about double my present capacity.), I went to both AtlasQuest and LBNA and didn't find any clues that mentioned Bridge to Nowhere (unless I really haven't mastered the search systems, which is another possibility), and found only "Rocky" the Squirrel Letterbox on LBNA (with a link to clues stored elsewhere). THAT turns out to be West Fork, so not even a possibility during this coming Saturday's hike.

Still, you my letterboxing friends (or some of you) do get out and hike occasionally or frequently, so any tips you can provide -- hiking, letterboxing, or otherwise -- would be appreciated.

Thanks,

Hansenclan
