New Old Finds

From: Kelsung (kelsung42@yahoo.com) | Date: 2012-01-08 23:50:37 UTC
Thanks to Dezert Ratty, who went and found my Duel box in Agua Dulce right where it was supposed to be, in spite of a couple reports to the contrary.

Of course, I've never let a few "attempts" deter me from looking for a box. I spent a lot of 2010 going after forgotten and retired boxes. I only have a few left on that list that have been sitting on my back-burner for a while, and I went after one last week.

After going to the new box planted near the Rose Bowl by out-of-towners visiting for the game, I headed to Lacy Park in San Marino for an old box by SpacyHolly. Guess what: in spite of the twisted branch mentioned in the clue being sawed off, the box was still underneath it, right where it was supposed to be.

Even with a lack of baggies, the stamp was in perfect shape and the totally full logbook had only minor moisture damage. I was the first to find it since August, 2006 almost 5 1/2 years ago.

Beyond that, thanks to the newest plants by MrOspital, I discovered that there are miles of trails off Turnbull Canyon Rd just waiting for letterboxes to be planted. 2012 is going to be a fun year.

Kelsung

From: jim54invc (chaosmanor47@gmail.com) | Date: 2012-01-09 16:16:21 UTC
Huh?

--- In letterboxing-socal@yahoogroups.com, Kim Flood wrote:

>... and Route 6 is not where it's supposed to be either -

You mean it doesn't end in Bishop, anymore? We were just up there last year, and the highway looked to be in good shape. Or were you referring to the fact that it doesn't go all the way into Long Beach, anymore? Not like that sort of thing doesn't happen a lot; some of us wish that the 101 still went all the way to San Diego, and that CA 1 still ran through Petrolia and the Lost Coast area.

Of course, quite a lot of the old Route 6 pavement still exists, as much as 95% of it, I would guess. With only a few small re-routes, there is nothing missing between Bishop and Mojave, and only a couple of miles is gone between Mojave and Rosamond, due to the bridge-connector over the RR tracks. Sierra Highway still runs about where it always has, and most of the Route's tracing through the LA Basin still exists. I can't be certain, as I've never taken the time to dig that deep, but I suspect that I've driven just about every mile of US 6 (The Grand Army of the Republic Highway) in California. Quite an interesting road, in some ways much more scenic and historic than Route 66 ever was, at least in California.

Of course, it wouldn't surprise me if CalTrans has mis-marked some of the old highway, i.e. put up "Historic Route 6" signs on the wrong road. Sacramento has been showing signs of dysfunction at least since the days of Pete Wilson, so if there are signs on the wrong road, well, it just fits a pattern that has manifested itself for decades. I'll have to look for them the next time I'm out that way. The only ones that I can be certain that I've seen are one on the 14 freeway, south of Palmdale, and one on Sierra Highway in the Saugus/Newhall area. I also know that I've seen one or two north of Inyokern, but I can't recall where.

Anyway, thanks for the memory jostle :-)

Jim, the other half of chaosmanor
