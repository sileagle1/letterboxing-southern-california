From: Kelsung (kelsung42@yahoo.com) | Date: 2012-03-04 00:07:08 UTC
Three things:

Sean of The Wheelers Have Landed from Seattle comes down our way on business once in a while. He will be in downtown LA at the end of March and I wanted to set up a dinnertime meet-and-greet the evening of Thursday the 29th. Who's in?

Mr. Ospital has posted an all day long "Carve In" so he can show us how to make fake rocks. He's hosting it at his home in La Habra and providing materials. The idea is we can start making them in the morning, and while the cement is drying, we can have an afternoon BBQ, then in the evening we should be able to paint them. Some who were interested can't make it on March 31st, so he's thinking of switching it to maybe April 15th. I wanted to help get the word out for him, so who's in?

Finally, I've waited long enough and have made a hasty decision on my location (which of course, I'm not yet revealing) so Kelsung Klue is here again. As I've mentioned, I wanted to give people who didn't get to play last time a chance to sign up first, then open any empty slots up to those who played last year. It will be on the last Saturday in April. Who's in? (Well, you'll have to wait a few weeks to actually sign up...)

Kelsung

From: Deanna (ethanksmom@aol.com) | Date: 2012-03-09 15:28:40 UTC
We have a lot of people who used to do Big Faire (or "that Dam Faire" as some people call it now), that's the grandmother of us all... It was at Agoura, then Glen Hellen, and now yeah, at the dam. In '98, we opened at Corona, our dates overlap some. It's definitely a different Faire, management prefers to call it a Festival, most of us call it a Faire-stival. ;-)

But yeah, it's different. Smaller, but growing every year. More intimate. All guild camps are open camps, you can come right up to our tents and houses, sit down at our tables, and everyone talks to you, there's a focus on educating AND entertaining the patrons. We have some great shows, a really good historical battle re-enactment, beer and food. Some of our vendors aren't as professional as at big faire, but that should be changing this year. We're the only Cali faire with permanent buildings, and the location is really nice, pretty now that we have more trees. It's more kid friendly, and you can bring your dogs!

Beach Celt
